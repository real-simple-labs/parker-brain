# Process — Audience Iteration

Change who is delivering or being targeted by the proven message, beyond a same-script creator reshoot. Audience iterations sit at the persona level of the signal-change ladder — a meaningful shift that opens new pockets of audience without rewriting the message.

The same-script creator reshoot has its own dedicated process. This one covers the broader audience-targeted moves.

## Required inputs

- The winning ad and its performance data.
- The full current script and creator brief.
- The brand's ICP, personas, target demographics, and any audiences the brand has explicitly tried to reach or explicitly avoided.
- Brand context including compliance and tone.

## Decision: which audience move

The audience menu, with when to pick each:

- **Greenscreen react.** New creator greenscreens the original ad and reacts to it, adding commentary. Feels native to TikTok and Reels. Low effort, medium reward.

- **Duet react.** New creator reacts to the original ad in side-by-side format. Can add a "STITCH INCOMING" headline to signal the reaction and improve retention. Low effort, medium reward.

- **AI avatar swap.** Use AI tools for low-cost creator testing before committing to full production. Use as exploratory before a real reshoot. Low effort, medium reward.

- **Demographic swap.** Same script delivered by creators from a different age, ethnicity, or lifestyle segment. Goal is reaching a new audience archetype with proven messaging. Medium effort, high reward.

- **Authority swap.** Replace a peer or relatable creator with an expert or credentialed figure — or the inverse, swap an authority figure for a peer. Use when the brand needs to test trust transfer or relatability shifts. Medium effort, high reward.

- **Voiceover swap.** Same visuals, different voiceover. Test gender, accent, energy level, or age of narrator. Low effort, medium reward.

- **AI voice vs human voice.** Test synthetic narration against real human voice. Low effort, medium reward.

- **Aspirational vs relatable creator.** Test someone the audience wants to be against someone the audience sees themselves as. Medium effort, high reward.

- **Persona variant.** Adjust language, examples, and pain points for a different buyer persona. Same product, different lens. Medium effort, high reward.

- **Gender variant.** Adjust creative elements to target a different gender demographic. Medium effort, medium reward.

- **Language translation.** Full translation with native speaker voiceover for international markets. Medium effort, high reward.

## Pre-check

- Is the audience expansion the actual goal, or is the current creator fatiguing within the existing audience? If it is fatigue within the existing audience, the same-script reshoot process is the right call, not this one.
- Does the brand have the casting access or production budget for the proposed move?
- Does the new audience match the product? Some products are not for all demographics. Be honest.
- For authority swaps: does the proposed authority figure actually have the credentials? Fake credentials are a compliance and credibility risk.

## Execution

1. **Name the current audience and the target audience explicitly.** Demographics, persona, lifestyle, awareness stage if relevant. Be specific.

2. **State which audience move from the menu.** One move per iteration.

3. **Define the new creator or voiceover brief precisely.** Age range, gender presentation if applicable, tone, energy, lifestyle signal, credentials if required. Vague briefs produce off-target deliveries.

4. **Identify what stays constant.** The script, the structure, the CTA, the key beats. If the persona variant requires small script adjustments (specific pain points referenced for the new persona), those are scoped adjustments, not a rewrite.

5. **Identify what is allowed to change.** Wardrobe, setting, b-roll, language register that matches the new audience's voice.

6. **Note compliance specifically for the new audience.** Language that works for one demographic can be inappropriate or non-compliant for another. Check.

## Output content for this iteration

- A name that signals the audience move. Example pattern: "Persona Variant — Same Script Adapted for [Target Persona]."
- The current audience and the target audience.
- The specific audience move from the menu.
- The new creator or voiceover brief.
- The "do not change" list.
- The "allowed to change" list.
- Any compliance notes specific to the new audience.
- The proven-pattern note tied to the chosen move.

## What never to do

- Never propose an audience iteration that targets a demographic the product does not serve.
- Never propose authority swaps with creators who do not have the actual credentials.
- Never let the new creator or voiceover drift the script into different messaging. This iteration tests audience, not message.
- Never combine an audience swap with a messaging change. Isolate the variable.
