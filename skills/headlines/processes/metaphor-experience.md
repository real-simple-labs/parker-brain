# Process — Metaphor / Experience Headlines

Make the product or its benefit concrete by linking it to something familiar the audience already understands. For lifestyle brands, metaphors capture the experience or vibe. For problem/solution brands, they make abstract benefits tangible.

## Structural patterns

The canonical structures and proven examples for this pattern live under "Metaphors & Similes" in both `lifestyle-headline-generator.md` and `problem-solution-headline-writer.md`. Those docs are the source of truth; pull the structures and examples from the one matching the brand's positioning. The shapes below orient the execution steps that follow.

- "Like X but for Y" — Like the gym but for sex.
- "Your X for Y" — Your GPS for money.
- "[Sensory] in a [format]" — Comfort in a cup.

## When to pick

- **Best for:** abstract benefits that are hard to describe directly. Categories where direct claims feel weak. Brands where the experience is the sell. Both brand types.
- **Worst for:** products where the benefit is already concrete and direct claims work better. Categories where metaphors feel forced.

## Execution

1. **Identify the core experience or benefit.** What is the primary thing the buyer wants to feel or get?
2. **Brainstorm familiar references.** What familiar object or experience elicits the same feeling? The reference must be universally understood by the target audience.
3. **Keep it simple but vivid.** "Comfort in a cup" lands. "Like a multifaceted tool that optimizes financial trajectories" does not.
4. **Under 10 words.**

## Output content

- The exact headline.
- The metaphor's source reference.
- The benefit or experience being made concrete.
- The ICP and the familiar reference the metaphor pulls from.

## What never to do

- Obscure metaphors that the audience does not immediately recognize.
- Metaphors that require explanation to land.
- Mixed metaphors or stretched comparisons.
