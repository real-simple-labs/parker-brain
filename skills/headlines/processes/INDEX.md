# Headlines — Process Index

Read this in order. The study and idea paths come first because a headline grounded in what already wins beats a clever structure built on air. The structural pattern library is the fallback for when a fresh structure is genuinely needed.

## Study first — the baseline

Run this before writing anything. The running corpus is the bar.

- **build-headline-baseline** — Pull the account's running titles, hooks, and headlines. Group them into families, read performance against them, extract the brand's fingerprint, and name the AI-tells by contrast. Mandatory first step.

## The idea paths — where the headline comes from

Pick the source before the structure. These are grounded in something real in the account, so they sound least AI-written.

- **headline-from-winning-headline** — Adapt a structure the account has already proven to a new angle or SKU. Structure holds, content swaps. The default first move.
- **headline-from-review-verbatim** — Lift a lift-ready customer line as written, governed by claims and voice rails.
- **headline-from-comment-response** — Answer a real ad comment in the headline. Name the objection in the customer's words.
- **headline-from-competitor-gap** — Make the claim a rival cannot. Spec-specific, receipt-backed, built from the vault's competitor evidence.
- **headline-from-stat-receipt** — A verified number as the engine. Review counts, study counts, under-claimed receipts. No-fabrication wall.

## The structural pattern library

Reach for these when the idea paths do not produce the line, or the brief needs a fresh structure. The structures themselves and the proven-example library live in the canonical docs — `lifestyle-headline-generator.md` and `problem-solution-headline-writer.md` — which are the source of truth. Each process below carries the execution playbook for one pattern and points back to the canonical doc for the structures and examples.

### Patterns for both brand types

- **rejection-contrast** — "Forget X," "F*ck the old way," "Stop Y," "Ditch Z."
- **metaphor-experience** — "Like X but for Y." Concrete-makes-abstract.
- **emotion-power-word** — Strong verbs and emotive triggers intensifying a benefit claim.

### Lifestyle-only patterns

- **identity-signal** — "Built for real X," "For people who Y."
- **badge-value-status** — "King of the office," exclusivity and status framing.
- **viral-fomo-appeal** — "Trying the viral X," only when real social proof exists.
- **reaction-discovery** — "I almost cried," "Blew my mind." The wow moment.
- **convenience-simplification** — "Goodbye lunch prep," "5-minute routine."

### Problem/solution-only patterns

- **problem-naming-direct** — Direct callout of the problem in customer language.
- **question-engagement** — Question form naming the problem ("Struggling with X?").
- **before-after-contrast** — Time and outcome contrast ("12 years gone in minutes").
- **promise-outcome** — Clear outcome promise ("No more burning").
- **duration-acknowledgment** — Investment framing for high-sophistication audiences.
- **authority-proof** — Credentialed validation ("Doctor-recommended," "#1 X").
- **alliteration-rhythm** — Memorability and stickiness ("Bye Bye Bloating").
- **parallel-structure** — Rhythm and contrast ("No X. No Y. Just Z.").
