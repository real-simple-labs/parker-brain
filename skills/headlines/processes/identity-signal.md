# Process — Identity-Signal Headlines (Lifestyle)

Help customers signal who they are by buying the brand. Tribal belonging — defines the in-group and makes the right person feel claimed.

## Structural patterns

The canonical structures and proven examples for this pattern live under "Identity & Belonging Signals" in `lifestyle-headline-generator.md`. That doc is the source of truth; pull the structures and examples from it. The shapes below orient the execution steps that follow.

- "Built for / Made for / Designed for [identity]" — Jeans built for real men, not just models.
- "Finally, [product] for [specific identity/experience]" — Finally, electrolytes WITHOUT stevia.
- Audience callouts — PSA: Hairy Girlies.
- "For people who [behavior or belief]" — For people who think clean.

## When to pick

- **Best for:** lifestyle brands. Identity-led purchases. Tribal audiences (Gen Z, niche communities, specific lifestyles). Brands defining a new category or audience.
- **Worst for:** problem/solution brands. Products without a clear identity dimension. Audiences who do not self-identify with the framing.

## Execution

1. **Lock the specific identity.** Not "men" — "real men who are tall enough that regular jeans look ridiculous." Not "women" — "women who refuse to shave."
2. **Pick the structural phrasing.** Built for / Finally / For people who / Audience callout.
3. **Make the identity exclusionary by implication.** The right person reads it and says "this is me"; the wrong person reads it and feels excluded — that exclusion is the feature.
4. **Under 10 words.**

## Output content

- The exact headline.
- The specific identity being claimed.
- The exclusion the headline implies.
- Why the in-group feel matches the brand's positioning.

## What never to do

- Generic identity callouts. "For everyone who loves great products" claims no identity.
- Forced identity language the brand voice does not support.
- Identity headlines on problem/solution brands where the buyer is pain-driven, not identity-driven.
