# Process — Promise / Outcome Headlines (Problem/Solution)

State the outcome the buyer wants in direct language. No setup, no question, no hedging. The promise IS the headline.

## Structural patterns

The canonical structures and proven examples for this pattern live in `problem-solution-headline-writer.md` — the make-a-promise move under "Set Up The Problem" and the "Feature vs. Benefit Balance" material. That doc is the source of truth; pull the structures and examples from it. The shapes below orient the execution steps that follow.

- "No more [pain]" — No more burning or tingling.
- "Last longer without [crutch]" — Last longer without pills.
- "Finally, [outcome]" — Finally, electrolytes WITHOUT stevia.
- "Say goodbye to [pain]" — Say goodbye to unwanted hair forever.

## When to pick

- **Best for:** problem/solution brands with clear, specific outcomes the product reliably delivers. Solution-aware and problem-aware audiences. Categories where the outcome is the lead motivator.
- **Worst for:** products with diffuse benefits. Categories where direct outcome claims violate compliance. Lifestyle brands.

## Execution

1. **Lock the outcome the buyer wants.** What is the specific transformation or relief?
2. **State it directly.** No hedging. No "may help." The promise is the lever.
3. **Match VoC language.** Pull the outcome phrasing from how customers describe what they want.
4. **Check compliance.** Outcome promises in health, finance, and medical categories have strict rules.

## Output content

- The exact headline.
- The outcome being promised.
- VoC source.
- Compliance flags.

## What never to do

- Hedged language. The promise must be direct.
- Outcome promises the product cannot reliably deliver.
- Marketing voice instead of customer voice.
- Vague outcomes. "Better skin" is too vague; "no more breakouts" is direct.
