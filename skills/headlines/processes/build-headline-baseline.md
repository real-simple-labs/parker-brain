# Process — Build the Headline Baseline (The Study Step)

Before writing a single headline, study what already wins in this account. The running corpus is the baseline. Adaptation of a proven structure beats invention, and generated headlines that were never measured against real winners are what sound AI-written. This process runs first, every time.

## What to pull

- The account's top spenders and top performers. For each, pull `ad_title`, `headline`, and `text_hook`. These three fields hold the real headline, the static overlay, and the scroll-stopping hook.
- Twenty or so per brand is enough to see the families. Pull both the highest-spend ads (what the team trusts with budget) and the highest-ROAS ads above a spend floor (what actually converts).
- The rival headlines captured in the vault. A competitor's framing is something to counter.

## Group into families

Sort the real headlines into the structures the account repeats. Real accounts run a handful of families, not infinite variety. The Perfect Jean leans on a persistent social-proof title — "35k+ 5-Star Reviews" rides nearly every top spender — plus blunt age callouts like "MEN OVER 50+ Your saggy dad jeans are aging you," fit-inclusivity lines like "Waists from 26 to 52 inches," and a comedy register that runs "These Khakis F#%K (Respectfully)" and "REGULAR KHAKIS SUCK. WE MADE THEM BETTER." Nutrafol runs a hope-and-consistency family — "CHANGE COMES FROM CONSISTENCY.", "Hair growth is possible for you." — a dupe-defense family — "DUPES COPY THE BOTTLE", "You can't dupe results." — and its single strongest evergreen, the side-effect counter "GET HAIR GAINS WITHOUT BEDROOM LOSSES." Liquid I.V. runs flavor-and-collab FOMO — "CHILL OUT. SIP A PICKLE.", "THE FLAVOR OF THE SUMMER" — and stacked offer lines — "BUY 3 GET 1 FREE", "LIMITED EDITION ONLINE EXCLUSIVE."

Name each family. These are the adaptation targets.

## Read performance against the families

Note which families carry the spend and which carry the ROAS. A family the team funds heavily is proven and safe to extend. A family with a high ROAS at lower spend is an underscaled winner worth pushing. The Grillo's Pickles collab line for Liquid I.V. ran ROAS above 10 — a register the account proved but has not flooded. Nutrafol's "GET HAIR GAINS WITHOUT BEDROOM LOSSES." carried both real spend and a 1.6+ ROAS — a proven evergreen, not a fluke.

## Extract the fingerprint

Write down internally, from the corpus, not from a generic notion of good copy:

- **Length.** How long are the winners? The Perfect Jean and Liquid I.V. run short and blunt; few exceed seven words.
- **Casing and punctuation.** All-caps callouts, sentence-case questions, the "#%K" self-censor, the period used as a full stop for emphasis. Match the account's habit.
- **Numbers and specifics.** "35k+", "Waists from 26 to 52 inches", "3-6 months", "BUY 3 GET 1 FREE." The winners name a number; vague headlines do not.
- **Register.** Comedy, hope, FOMO, authority. One account's register is wrong for another.

## Name the AI-tells by contrast

Read against two contrast sets. The first is the canonical AI-tells list in `spoken-script-voice.md` — it applies to headlines, not just scripts, and it is the source of truth for the tells. The second is this account's own corpus: whatever the winning headlines never do, forbid it. Generated headlines reach for a colon-summary cadence, "Discover / Unlock / Elevate / Transform" verb openers, balanced rule-of-three triads invented for rhythm, hedge phrases like "say goodbye to" or "the secret to," and the marketing abstraction standing in for the named thing. None of these appear in the corpus above. The real winners are blunter and more specific. If a candidate carries a tell the corpus does not, it is wrong on its face.

## Output of this step

A short internal note: the named families, which carries spend and which carries ROAS, the fingerprint, and the AI-tells to avoid. Carry this into every downstream process. The headlines you write reference it in the "Baseline Studied" block.

## What never to do

- Skip the study and write from a generic sense of "good headline." That is the failure mode this process exists to kill.
- Invent a family the account has never run when a proven one fits the brief.
- Treat one brand's fingerprint as portable. Each account's register is its own.
