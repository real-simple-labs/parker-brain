# Process — Parallel Structure Headlines

Repetition of phrases or grammatical structures to create rhythm and contrast. The pattern is "No X. No Y. Just Z." or "More X. Less Y." or similar.

## Structural patterns

The canonical structures and proven examples for this pattern live under "Proven Word Structures, Formats And Rhythms" in both `lifestyle-headline-generator.md` and `problem-solution-headline-writer.md`. Those docs are the source of truth; pull the structures and examples from the one matching the brand's positioning. The shapes below orient the execution steps that follow.

- "No X. No Y. Just Z." — No bloat. No crash. Just clean energy.
- "Less X. More Y." — Less time prepping. More time eating.
- "X without Y" — Long-lasting without irritation.
- "X, not Y" — Jeans built for real men, not just models.

## When to pick

- **Best for:** statements that contrast benefit with avoided drawback. Categories where multiple selling points need to fit in one line. Both brand types.
- **Worst for:** single-benefit products where parallel structure forces a contrast that does not exist.

## Execution

1. **Identify the benefit and the avoided drawback.** What does the brand deliver, and what does it specifically NOT do?
2. **Pick the structural shape.** "No X. No Y. Just Z." is the strongest for clean energy claims. "X, not Y" lands harder for identity-led contrasts.
3. **Keep elements parallel grammatically.** "No bloat. No crash. Just clean energy" — all noun phrases. Mixed structures break the rhythm.
4. **Under 10 words.**

## Output content

- The exact headline.
- The benefit being claimed.
- The avoided drawback being contrasted.
- Why this structure fits the brand.

## What never to do

- Forced parallel structures where the elements are not actually parallel.
- More than three repeating elements. Reader fatigue.
- Parallel structure where there is no real contrast.
