# Process — Before/After Contrast Headlines (Problem/Solution)

Contrast a specific duration of pain with the speed or completeness of the solution. Specific numbers and specific timeframes are the levers.

## Structural patterns

The canonical structures and proven examples for this pattern live in `problem-solution-headline-writer.md` — the contrast move under "Emotional Resonance" and the neuropathy and compression-sock good examples. That doc is the source of truth; pull the structures and examples from it. The shapes below orient the execution steps that follow.

- "[Duration] of [pain] gone in [shorter duration]" — 12 years of nerve pain gone in 5 minutes.
- "[Specific bad state] to [specific good state]" — From restless nights to deep sleep.
- "[Number] [duration] without [pain]" — 14+ hours. No swelling.

## When to pick

- **Best for:** problem/solution brands with quick, measurable transformations. Categories with strong before-and-after potential. Solution-aware audiences who have been waiting for something that works.
- **Worst for:** products with slow or invisible benefits. Categories where before-and-after claims violate compliance (especially health, weight, medical). Lifestyle brands.

## Execution

1. **Pull the duration from VoC.** "12 years of foot pain" is real; "lots of years" is not.
2. **Lock the contrast.** Specific number to specific number, specific timeframe to specific timeframe.
3. **Check compliance.** Before-and-after claims have strict rules in health and weight categories.
4. **Under 10 words.**

## Output content

- The exact headline.
- The duration and outcome specifics.
- Compliance flags if any.
- The ICP and the transformation desire.

## What never to do

- Vague durations or outcomes. Specificity is the lever.
- Inflated timeframes that strain credibility.
- Before-and-after claims that violate platform compliance.
- Promise outcomes the product cannot deliver.
