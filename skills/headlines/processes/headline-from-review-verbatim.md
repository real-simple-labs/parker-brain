# Process — Headline From a Review, Verbatim (Lift-As-Written)

Lift a customer line as written and use it as the headline, almost untouched. The mining method flags lift-ready golden nuggets — phrasing so specific and human a strategist could not have invented it. These are the headlines that sound least AI-written, because a customer wrote them.

## When to pick

- **Best for:** brands with vivid, specific reviews. Apparel, supplements, anything where customers describe a felt result. Social-proof and most-aware stages where a real voice converts harder than brand voice.
- **Worst for:** categories where reviews are generic ("love it," "great product"). A bland review is not a lift-ready nugget. Compliance-heavy brands where customer claims trip the rails.

## What makes a line lift-ready

- It is specific and visual. It paints a picture or names a felt result, not a vague endorsement.
- It uses the customer's own register — slang, imperfect grammar, the turn of phrase a friend would text.
- It carries an emotional charge or a reaction. The mining method flags these as golden nuggets.
- It is not a testimonial-trap phrase. "Best ever," "so good," "worth it" are not nuggets; they are filler.

## Execution

1. **Pull from real reviews.** Use the lift-ready nuggets the mining method flagged. Do not paraphrase a generic review into something that sounds quotable — that is invention wearing a customer costume.
2. **Lift it nearly as-is.** Trim to under 10 words, fix only what blocks comprehension. Keep the customer's grammar and slang. The imperfection is the proof.
3. **Run the claims governor.** A customer can say things the brand cannot. If the lifted line makes a claim that violates compliance — a health outcome, a guarantee, a superiority claim the brand is barred from — it cannot run, even though a customer said it. Flag it and offer a compliant lift instead.
4. **Run the voice governor.** The line must still sound on-brand. A curse-word nugget works for some blunt comedy registers and not for a clinical brand.
5. **Attribute it.** Note it is a verbatim customer line, with the review it came from.

## Output content

- The exact headline.
- The review it was lifted from, quoted, with the trim noted if any.
- The claim or voice check it passed.
- The ICP and emotion.

## What never to do

- Invent a "customer-sounding" line. If no review said it, it is not a verbatim lift — it is fabrication.
- Polish the grammar until it sounds like marketing. The roughness is the asset.
- Lift a line that breaks compliance because a customer said it. The rails hold regardless of source.
- Lift a testimonial-trap phrase. "So good" is not a headline.
