# Process — Headline From a Competitor Gap (The Claim a Rival Cannot Make)

Build a headline around a spec-specific, receipt-backed claim a competitor cannot match. The competitor's own framing is the setup; the spec they lack is the punch. This is a counter-headline, grounded in the competitor evidence captured in the vault. Nutrafol already runs the shape: "GET HAIR GAINS WITHOUT BEDROOM LOSSES." is a counter to finasteride's sexual-side-effect reputation, and it was the account's strongest evergreen performer.

## When to pick

- **Best for:** crowded categories with a clear adversary. Solution-aware audiences who have tried the competitor and felt its weakness. Brands with a real spec advantage the receipts back.
- **Worst for:** categories with no clear rival, or where the brand's advantage is parity, not a gap. Never manufacture a competitor weakness the receipts do not support.

## Execution

1. **Find the gap in the vault.** Pull the rival headlines and competitor evidence captured in the vault. What does the competitor claim, and what can they not say? The gap is a spec they lack, a side effect they carry, an ingredient they miss, a fit they do not offer.
2. **Anchor the counter in a real spec.** The counter only works if the brand genuinely holds the advantage and the receipts prove it. "Without bedroom losses" lands because Nutrafol is a supplement, not a DHT-blocking drug. The Perfect Jean's "Waists from 26 to 52 inches" is a quiet counter to brands that stop at a 40.
3. **Name the gap, not the competitor.** Usually you contrast against the category weakness rather than naming the rival. The audience fills in who you mean.
4. **Keep the receipt attached.** If the counter rests on a number or a study, the no-fabrication wall applies — real figures only.
5. **Under 10 words.**

## Output content

- The exact headline.
- The competitor claim or weakness it counters, sourced to the vault evidence.
- The brand spec that backs the counter, and its receipt.
- The ICP and emotion.

## What never to do

- Invent a competitor weakness the evidence does not support. The gap must be real.
- Make a counter-claim the brand cannot actually back. The spec advantage has to hold.
- Name and attack a competitor where the brand voice or legal rails forbid it.
- Counter on parity. If both brands can claim it, it is not a gap.
