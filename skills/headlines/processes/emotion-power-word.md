# Process — Emotion / Power Word Headlines

Intensify a benefit claim with strong verbs and emotive triggers. The pattern is taking a flat benefit statement and replacing weak words with words that activate emotion.

## Power word categories

The canonical power-word categories and the upgrade-your-language method live under "Power Word Categories" and "Upgrade Your Language" in both `lifestyle-headline-generator.md` and `problem-solution-headline-writer.md`. Those docs are the source of truth; pull the full category lists from the one matching the brand's positioning. The categories below orient the execution steps that follow.

- **Fear:** Agony, Disastrous, Burnout, Exposed.
- **Encouragement:** Life-changing, Transformative, Miraculous, Empower.
- **Anger:** Brutal, Vicious, Fed up, Crush.
- **Lust/Desire:** Alluring, Irresistible, Spellbinding, Seductive.
- **Greed:** Skyrocket, No cost, Profit instantly.
- **Safety:** Foolproof, Data-backed, Doctor-approved, Science-based.
- **Pride:** Elevated, Premium, Discerning, Refined.

## When to pick

- **Best for:** flat benefit statements that need emotional charge. Both brand types. When the existing headline reads as muted and the audience needs a sharper emotional signal.
- **Worst for:** brands whose voice is restrained. Categories where intense emotional language reads as overselling.

## Execution

1. **Start with the flat benefit.** Identify the underlying claim or transformation.
2. **Pick the dominant emotion.** What is the buyer responding to?
3. **Replace weak words with power words.** "Exogenous ketones help you ease into keto and remain in ketosis" becomes "KICKSTART ketosis and SUSTAIN it."
4. **Crank it up to 11.** Ask: how can this be bolder, clearer, more polarizing without losing the brand voice?

## Output content

- The original flat claim (if iterating).
- The power-charged version.
- The emotional category being activated.
- The ICP and the emotion.

## What never to do

- Power words on brands whose voice does not support them.
- Stack too many power words. Two intensifiers max per headline.
- Power words without an underlying real claim. Hype without substance breaks trust.
