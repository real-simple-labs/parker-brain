# Process — Viral / FOMO Appeal Headlines (Lifestyle)

Activate bandwagon effect by signaling that the product is trending or being widely adopted. Only when the social proof is real.

## Structural patterns

The canonical structures, proven examples, and the when-to-use-virality rules for this pattern live under "Viral & Social Proof Signals" in `lifestyle-headline-generator.md`. That doc is the source of truth; pull the structures and examples from it. The shapes below orient the execution steps that follow.

- "Trying the viral X."
- "Everyone's talking about Y."
- "[Number] people can't be wrong."
- "TikTok made me buy it."
- "I finally tried [the trending X]."
- "What [TikTok / Instagram / the internet] is obsessed with."

## When to pick

- **Best for:** products with real social momentum. Strong review volume (1,000+). Organic social presence. Cultural moments and trending categories. Impulse purchase categories. Lifestyle brands.
- **Worst for:** products without real virality. Premium brands where mass adoption undermines exclusivity. Categories where the audience is older or trend-resistant.

## Execution

1. **Verify the social proof.** Real review counts, real follower numbers, real platform momentum. If the proof does not exist, pick a different structure.
2. **Pick the framing.** Discovery ("I finally tried") vs mass validation ("X people can't be wrong") vs platform reference ("TikTok made me").
3. **Use specific numbers when available.** "Over 2.5 million users" reads real; "millions of users" reads invented.
4. **Pair with visible proof, grounded.** When this headline runs in a static or video, the visual should reinforce the social proof. Source the paired visual from the brand's in-play shots in `visual-vocabulary.md` per `visual-vocabulary-method.md`; flag any visual the brand has not actually filmed as out-of-play rather than directing invented stock.

## Output content

- The exact headline.
- The real social proof being cited, with sources.
- The platform or context the virality references.
- The ICP and the FOMO trigger.

## What never to do

- Claim virality that does not exist.
- Round numbers that read as invented.
- Use on premium brands whose exclusivity is the lever.
- Skip the source. Every claim must trace to verified data.
