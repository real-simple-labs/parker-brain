# Process — Duration Acknowledgment Headlines (Problem/Solution)

Acknowledge the audience's investment — years tried, money spent, solutions failed — to build rapport with high-sophistication buyers who have been burned before.

## Structural patterns

The canonical material for this pattern lives in `problem-solution-headline-writer.md` — the customer-sophistication guidance and the duration-contrast good examples. That doc is the source of truth; pull the structures and examples from it. The shapes below orient the execution steps that follow.

- "[Duration] of [pain]" — 12 years of foot pain.
- "After trying [number] [solutions]" — After trying 14 different supplements.
- "[Specific failed attempt] before I found this."
- "I spent $X on Y before I found Z."

## When to pick

- **Best for:** high-sophistication markets where the audience has tried 3+ solutions. Categories with many competing fixes. Audiences for whom direct claims feel like another empty promise.
- **Worst for:** low-sophistication audiences just discovering the problem. Categories with no clear failed-attempt history.

## Execution

1. **Pull the duration or investment from real reviews.** Customers who write "I tried X, Y, and Z before this" are giving you the headline.
2. **Be specific.** Specific years, specific failed solutions, specific dollar amounts.
3. **Use first-person or implied-first-person voice.** This pattern works when it sounds like the customer is telling their story.
4. **Pair the acknowledgment with a transformation implied or stated.** The acknowledgment is half the headline; the relief is the other half.

## Output content

- The exact headline.
- The duration and prior attempts cited.
- VoC source for the specific failures.
- Why this fits the sophistication level of the target audience.

## What never to do

- Generic "I tried everything" framings. Specificity is the lever.
- Duration acknowledgment on low-sophistication audiences who do not have the prior-attempt history.
- Invented prior-attempt stories. Source from real reviews.
