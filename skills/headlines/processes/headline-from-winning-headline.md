# Process — Headline From a Winning Headline (Adaptation)

Adapt a structure this account has already proven to a new angle, product, or SKU. The structure holds; the content swaps. This is the default first move whenever the baseline has a winner that fits the brief. A headline built on a proven structure carries the account's voice automatically, which is exactly what generated-from-scratch headlines miss.

## When to pick

- **Best for:** any brief where the baseline study surfaced a winning family that fits. New SKU launch on a brand with a strong format. A fresh angle that can ride an existing structure. The safe-and-proven option in any set.
- **Worst for:** a genuinely new positioning the account has never run, where no proven structure fits. Then route to an idea path or a structural pattern instead.

## Execution

1. **Pick the winner to adapt.** From the baseline families, choose the structure that fits the new angle. Prefer a family that carries both spend and ROAS, or an underscaled high-ROAS winner worth pushing.
2. **Isolate what makes it work.** Is it the all-caps blunt callout, the number, the self-censor joke, the side-effect counter? Name the mechanism, not just the words.
3. **Swap the content, keep the mechanism.** Liquid I.V.'s "THE FLAVOR OF THE SUMMER" is a seasonal-superlative structure; a new flavor drop adapts to the same shape. Nutrafol's "DUPES COPY THE BOTTLE" is an original-versus-imitator structure; a new competitor claim rides it. The Perfect Jean's "REGULAR KHAKIS SUCK. WE MADE THEM BETTER" is a category-rejection-plus-fix structure; a new product line adapts the same two-beat move.
4. **Re-run the fingerprint.** Match the original's length, casing, and punctuation. If the winner was all-caps and seven words, the adaptation is all-caps and seven words.
5. **Under 10 words.**

## Output content

- The exact headline.
- The winning headline it adapts, quoted exactly, and the mechanism it borrows.
- The new angle or SKU it applies to.
- The ICP and emotion.

## What never to do

- Copy the winner's content instead of its structure. Adaptation swaps the content.
- Adapt a structure whose register is wrong for the new angle.
- Drop the fingerprint. An adaptation that changes the casing or length stops sounding like the family it came from.
- Adapt a low performer because it was easy to find. Pull from proven winners.
