# Process — Script From Organic Niche

Adapt a breakout organic video — a TikTok, Reel, or Short with real view and engagement signal — into a paid script for the brand. Mine the brand's niche library, pick the breakout, run the 1:1 adaptation, and honor the honesty rules.

The organic view count is the validation. The adaptation is structural fidelity, not reinvention.

## Required inputs

- A breakout organic video from the brand's niche with view and engagement signal. Source: the mined organic library via the `search_and_manage_organic_social` MCP surface and `creative-strategy-context/organic-social-analysis.md`.
- Brand baseline — the brand's existing scripts.
- Brand profile, ICP, voice of customer, compliance constraints.
- The video's transcript, segment map, and on-screen text.

## Philosophy

Roughly a third of the time a breakout organic video is a full concept worth adapting one to one. The rest of the time the find is narrower — a hook that stops the scroll, a format, a messaging beat — and only that piece travels. Either way the organic engagement is the verified hunch that this shape works in the niche. The adaptation method is `creative-strategy-context/adapting-scripts.md`: select one reference, deconstruct it, build a 1:1 segment skeleton, replace content not structure.

The honesty rule governs hacks. When the breakout works because of a hack or trick, run it honestly — show what the product actually does — then retire it. A dishonest demo that goes wide burns trust faster than it converts.

## When to pick

- A breakout organic video exists in the niche with engagement that signals the concept works.
- The brand's solution slots into the concept cleanly.
- The user wants to ride proven organic momentum into paid.

## Execution

### Step 1 — Confirm the breakout qualifies
Weigh views and engagement against post date. High views on an old post is signal; low views on a fresh post is just young. Confirm the brand's solution inserts into the concept without forcing it.

### Step 2 — Classify the find
Full concept, or a piece. If full concept, adapt the whole thing 1:1. If a piece — a hook, a format — carry only that and build the rest in the brand baseline.

### Step 3 — Deconstruct and skeleton per adapting-scripts.md
Segment map, hook type, on-screen text pattern, proof style, CTA. Match the segment count exactly. Preserve text-on-screen-only classification if the original had no voiceover.

### Step 4 — Replace content, source from VoC
Swap the niche product for ours, the niche language for our customer's words from real reviews and comments. Structure stays; content becomes ours.

### Step 5 — Honesty pass
If the concept rode a hack, rebuild the demo to show what the product honestly does, then flag the hack as one-and-done. Never carry a misleading demonstration into paid.

## What good looks like

- The adapted paid script reads as the same video in the brand's world.
- The find is correctly classified — full concept versus single piece — and only what should travel travels.
- Any hack is run honestly and marked for retirement.
- Customer language throughout, segment count matched.

## Common failures

- Adapting a low-engagement organic with no validation behind it.
- Expanding or compressing the segment count. Pacing drift is structural failure.
- Adding voiceover to a text-on-screen-only original.
- Running a dishonest hack because it tested well organically.
