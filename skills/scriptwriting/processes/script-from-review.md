# Process — Script From Review

A golden-nugget customer review becomes the spine of the script. The verbatim is the hook or the testimony. The reviewer's own arc — trigger, doubt, verdict — is the beat structure.

The review already converted one customer in their own words. The script is that conversion, staged.

## Required inputs

- A high-signal review with a clear arc. Source: the customer review audit and VoC docs — `creative-strategy-context/customer-review-mining-method.md`, the `search_customer_reviews_semantic` MCP surface, and the VoC corpus at `prompts/voice-of-customer/`.
- Brand baseline — the brand's existing scripts.
- Brand profile, ICP, compliance constraints.

## Philosophy

A review is unscripted persuasion that already worked. The customer named their trigger, voiced their doubt, and delivered their verdict in language no marketer would write. That arc is a ready-made beat structure, and the verbatim phrasing is the most credible hook the brand owns because it is real.

Two builds. The verbatim as hook: the review's sharpest line read on camera opens the ad. The arc as structure: the reviewer's trigger becomes the problem beat, their doubt becomes the objection beat, their verdict becomes the proof and close. Either way the script speaks in the review's words, not the brand's.

## When to pick

- A review exists with a clean, dramatizable arc and a quotable line.
- The brand wants testimony-grade credibility.
- Solution-aware or product-aware audiences who respond to social proof.

## Execution

### Step 1 — Pull the golden-nugget review
Find the review with a sharp verbatim line and a complete arc — what triggered the search, what doubt they carried, what verdict they reached. Pull it word for word.

### Step 2 — Choose the build
Verbatim-as-hook, or arc-as-structure. If one line carries unusual punch, lead with it. If the whole arc is strong, map it beat for beat.

### Step 3 — Map the arc to beats
Trigger → problem beat. Doubt → objection beat. Verdict → proof and close. Keep the reviewer's sequence; do not reorder their emotional journey.

### Step 4 — Keep the language verbatim where it lands
The review's actual phrasing carries the credibility. Preserve the exact words in the hook and the verdict. Smooth only for readability, never into marketing voice.

### Step 5 — Brand baseline and compliance pass
Match the brand's pacing and CTA style. Confirm the review makes no claim the brand cannot stand behind; if it does, swap for the nearest compliant sensory or experience claim in the same beat.

## What good looks like

- The hook or verdict is the reviewer's actual words.
- The beat structure follows the reviewer's real arc: trigger, doubt, verdict.
- The script reads as one customer's honest story, not a brand pitch.
- Pacing and CTA match the brand baseline.

## Common failures

- Polishing the verbatim into marketing voice and losing the credibility.
- Reordering the arc so it no longer matches a real emotional journey.
- Pulling a thin review with no arc and forcing a structure onto it.
- Carrying a review claim the brand cannot defend. Compliance is a wall even when a customer said it.
