# Process — Script From Persona Lifecycle

Pick the persona. Pick the lifecycle stage. Write the script in that persona's documented diction at that exact moment in their journey — the relapse-restart win-back, the gift-buyer in her own voice, the three-month-verdict reassurance.

The persona voice library and the lifecycle journey maps together fix who is speaking and when. The script is that person at that beat.

## Required inputs

- The persona voice library. Source: `prompts/personas/persona-voice-library.md`.
- The lifecycle journey maps. Source: `prompts/personas/lifecycle-journey-maps.md`.
- Brand baseline — the brand's existing scripts.
- Brand profile, compliance constraints.

## Philosophy

A persona is not a demographic. It is a documented voice — a diction, a set of concerns, a way of talking. A lifecycle stage is a moment in that person's relationship with the product — first search, first purchase, three-month verdict, lapse, win-back. The same persona sounds different at different stages, and the same stage sounds different across personas. Fixing both coordinates locks the exact register the script must hold.

The win is specificity. A script written for "the customer" speaks to no one. A script written for the relapse-restart customer at the win-back moment, in her documented diction, speaks to exactly one person — and that is who converts.

## When to pick

- The persona voice library and lifecycle maps are built for the brand.
- The campaign targets a specific persona at a specific journey stage — win-back, gifting, post-purchase reassurance.
- The brand wants register precision over broad reach.

## Execution

### Step 1 — Pick the persona and the stage
Choose one persona from the voice library and one stage from the journey map. The pair is the script's coordinates.

### Step 2 — Pull the documented diction
From the voice library, pull how this persona actually talks — their words, their concerns, their tone. This is the script's voice, not the brand's house voice.

### Step 3 — Pull the stage's emotional state
From the journey map, pull what this persona feels at this stage. The relapse-restart customer carries guilt and hope. The gift-buyer thinks in someone else's voice. The three-month customer wants reassurance the result holds.

### Step 4 — Write at the intersection
Hook, body, and close all sit at the persona-stage intersection. The relapse-restart win-back forgives and invites back. The gift-buyer script is written in her voice about the recipient. The three-month-verdict script reassures the result is real.

### Step 5 — Brand baseline and compliance pass
Match pacing and CTA to the brand baseline while keeping the persona's diction. Confirm no claim exceeds what the brand can defend.

## What good looks like

- The script reads as one specific persona at one specific stage.
- The diction is the persona's documented voice, not the brand house voice.
- The emotional state matches the lifecycle moment.
- Pacing and CTA still match the brand baseline.

## Common failures

- Writing for "the customer" instead of one persona at one stage.
- Using the brand house voice where the persona's documented diction should govern.
- Mismatching diction and stage — the win-back voice on a first-purchase script.
- A claim the brand cannot defend slipped into a persona's mouth.
