# Process — Write Storytelling Script

Organic story-arc framework. Intro → conflict → rising action → climax → resolution. Reads as content, not as a DR pitch. The product enters as the resolution to the journey, not as the opening claim.

## Required inputs

- Brand profile, ICP, voice of customer.
- A real story or composite story the brand can tell — founder journey, customer transformation, discovery moment.
- Brand voice — storytelling needs a register the brand can carry authentically.
- Format length.

## When to pick

- **Best for:** unaware and problem-aware audiences. Longer-form content. Founder content. Testimonials and case studies. Lifestyle brands. Categories where the audience tunes out DR voice. Meta's algorithm favors creative diversity across funnel stages, and storytelling scripts deliver that diversity.
- **Worst for:** quick-conversion impulse categories. Short-form formats with limited time for setup. Audiences who want the offer fast.

## Structure

1. **Opening in medias res (0-5 seconds).** Start mid-story. "5 years ago my doctor told me..." or "I was 28 when I realized..."
2. **Conflict (5-15 seconds).** The struggle. What was wrong. What was at stake.
3. **Rising action (15-30 seconds).** The journey. Failed attempts, dead ends, frustration. This is where investment framing fits.
4. **Climax / discovery (30-40 seconds).** The moment of finding the solution. The reveal.
5. **Resolution (40-end).** What life looks like now. The transformation. The product naturally fits here as the answer.

## Execution

1. **Start the story specifically.** Specific time markers, specific stakes, specific characters. Generic openings ("I always loved...") fail.
2. **Use perspective shifts.** The character learns something, discovers something, changes their mind. Each shift is a dopamine hit that holds the viewer.
3. **Build on surprises.** When a surprising moment lands, do not let it fizzle. Lean into it.
4. **Pace the setup.** 15-40 seconds of setup before the solution is appropriate for longer-form. Shorter formats need tighter pacing.
5. **Place the product in the middle of the resolution.** The product is not the hero of the opening — it is the answer at the end.
6. **Close with the transformation in customer voice.**

## Output content

- The full story script with timing.
- Visual cues per beat — reflective opening, journey beats, climax, resolution.
- The hook details for the opening moment.
- Where and how the product enters.

## What never to do

- Generic story openings without specific stakes or time markers.
- Make the product the hero of the opening. It enters as the resolution.
- Drag the setup past what the format supports.
- Storytelling on impulse categories with no time for a story arc.
