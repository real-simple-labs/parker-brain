# Process — Write Awareness-Stage Script

Script built around the audience's specific awareness stage. The stage determines the structural lead, the depth of education, and the role the product plays in the narrative.

## Required inputs

- The audience's awareness stage — unaware, problem-aware, solution-aware, product-aware, or most-aware.
- Brand profile, ICP, voice of customer.
- Format length.

## Stage-specific approaches

### Unaware
The audience does not know the problem exists. Lead with storytelling or hidden-villain reveals. Educate slowly. The product appears late, as the natural extension of the discovery.

- Hook: storytelling or curiosity gap.
- Body: discovery arc revealing the problem the audience did not know they had.
- Product: introduced as the answer once the problem is named.
- Pacing: longer setup acceptable.

### Problem-aware
Audience knows the problem, does not know solutions exist. Educate on solutions and differentiate.

- Hook: problem naming or "how do I know if" question.
- Body: brief problem acknowledgment, then education on solution categories, then the specific mechanism this product uses.
- Product: introduced in the middle as the right solution category, with mechanism differentiation.
- Pacing: medium.

### Solution-aware
Audience has tried solutions that failed. Acknowledge the failures and differentiate hard.

- Hook: investment hook, "I tried X for Y years," scam hook, or false-hope lead.
- Body: failed-attempt acknowledgment, mechanism reveal explaining why this works when others did not, proof from people with similar journeys.
- Product: introduced after failed-attempt acknowledgment with a clear mechanism distinction.
- Pacing: medium to longer for VSL contexts.

### Product-aware
Audience knows the product, considering it. Lean on social proof, comparison, urgency.

- Hook: social proof, urgency, comparison.
- Body: stack proof, address remaining objections, anchor the offer.
- Product: assumed known — the script is about closing the consideration gap.
- Pacing: shorter.

### Most-aware
Audience is ready to buy. Direct CTA, offer urgency, scarcity.

- Hook: offer-led or scarcity-led.
- Body: minimal — repeat the offer, add urgency, close.
- Product: assumed bought-into.
- Pacing: very short.

## Execution

1. **Confirm the stage from VoC and brand data.** Look at how customers describe their journey when they encounter the brand. Are they discovering the problem, comparing solutions, or already ready?
2. **Pick the stage-specific approach above.**
3. **Write the script following the stage-appropriate structure.**
4. **Verify the structure matches the stage.** An unaware-targeted script that opens with a direct offer is mismatched. A most-aware-targeted script that opens with education wastes time.

## Output content

- The full script with timing.
- Visual cues per beat.
- The awareness stage targeted with rationale.
- VoC sources confirming the audience is at this stage.

## What never to do

- Write a script that does not match the audience's actual stage.
- Treat awareness stages as fixed categories — audiences move through stages. Pick the dominant stage but acknowledge if the audience straddles two.
- Skip the education layer for problem-aware audiences. Skip the proof layer for solution-aware audiences. Each stage has its non-negotiables.
