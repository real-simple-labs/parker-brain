# Process — Demographic Breakdown

Read what age, gender, or combined demographic breakdowns reveal about who Meta is actually reaching, whether that matches the brand's intended ICP, and whether a mismatch is hurting performance.

## Required inputs

- Age, gender, or age-and-gender combined breakdown of spend, CPA, ROAS, frequency, CTR, CvR.
- The brand's intended ICP — primary and secondary personas — and the age and gender mix the brand expects to reach.
- The brand's primary KPI.

## Execution

1. **Sort by spend.** Meta is showing where it thinks the audience is. The demographic absorbing the most spend is the one the algorithm has converged on.

2. **Compare to ICP expectation.** If the brand expects to reach 30-45-year-old women but spend is concentrating on 50-65-year-old men, that is a major mismatch worth understanding. Either the creative is reading older/male, the targeting is broader than intended, or the brand's actual buyer is different from who they think their buyer is.

3. **Read profitability by demographic.** Which demographics are converting profitably? Which are absorbing spend but not converting? Which are converting at sub-target CPA?

4. **Read engagement by demographic.** If hook rate or hold rate breaks out by age/gender, surface it. Older audiences typically support longer ads with slower pacing. Younger audiences need faster cuts.

5. **Identify the strategic implication.** A mismatch between intended ICP and converting demographic is not automatically a problem — sometimes the algorithm has found an audience the brand did not know they had. State whether the read suggests adjusting the creative, adjusting the targeting, or rethinking the ICP.

## Output

- **The Headline Read.** Who is actually being reached and converting, whether that matches the ICP, and what to do about any gap.
- **Demographic-by-Demographic Read.** Per relevant demographic slice: spend, CPA, ROAS, engagement signals, and what they imply.
- **ICP Fit Diagnosis.** Whether the spend distribution matches the brand's intended ICP. Mismatch flagged with explanation.
- **Recommended Next Steps.** Whether to adjust creative for the actual converting demographic, adjust targeting, or expand the brand's ICP definition.

## What never to do

- Never assume the brand's stated ICP is correct. The data is the ground truth. If the brand expects 30-year-olds but converts 60-year-olds at lower CPA, the 60-year-olds are part of the ICP whether the brand recognized it or not.
- Never recommend narrowing targeting just because a demographic is "off-ICP" if that demographic is profitable.
- Never read demographic data without the creative context. Older audiences absorbing more spend probably means the creative is reading older — that is information about the creative, not just the targeting.
