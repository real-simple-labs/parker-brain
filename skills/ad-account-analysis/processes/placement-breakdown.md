# Process — Placement Breakdown

Read what the placement distribution implies about the audience being reached, where spend is concentrating and why, and whether the placement mix matches the strategic goal.

## Required inputs

- Placement-level breakdown of spend, CPA, ROAS, frequency, CPM for the account or for a specific ad/campaign.
- The brand's intended audience and where they live (older skew → Facebook, younger → Instagram Reels/Stories).
- The brand's funnel goals — top-of-funnel reach, mid-funnel consideration, bottom-funnel conversion.

## Execution

1. **Sort placements by spend.** Meta is showing you where it thinks the audience is. The biggest placement absorbs the most spend because the algorithm predicts the lowest cost outcomes there.

2. **Apply the placement signals from strategy.md.** Each placement implies a different audience and intent profile:
   - Facebook Feed → older, community-oriented, consideration/research phase.
   - Instagram Feed → younger, aspirational, top/mid funnel, responds to visual cues.
   - Facebook Reels → casual viewing, broader/older, top-of-funnel reach with weaker intent.
   - Instagram Reels → trend-following, younger, early funnel, high virality potential.
   - Facebook Stories → older, passive tapping-through, upper-funnel exposure.
   - Instagram Stories → younger, interactive, mid-to-lower funnel if conversion CTAs work.

3. **Compare placement performance to overall account.** Where is each placement delivering better or worse than the account average? CPM, CPA, ROAS, frequency.

4. **Diagnose mismatch.** If the brand wants younger but Facebook Feed is absorbing spend, the algorithm is telling you something — either the creative reads as Facebook-skew (older), or the targeting is broader than intended, or the audience the brand thinks they want is not where their content is actually converting.

5. **Read the breakdown effect.** A placement absorbing more spend with a higher current CPA is not necessarily Meta misallocating — Meta may have predicted that placement's costs would rise more slowly than alternatives over the campaign duration. Account for it before flagging.

## Output

- **The Headline Read.** What the placement mix is saying about who is being reached and whether that matches the goal.
- **Placement-by-Placement Read.** For each meaningful placement: spend share, performance, what it implies about the audience.
- **Mismatch Flags.** Any placement absorbing spend that does not match the brand's intended audience or funnel position.
- **Recommended Next Steps.** Whether the brand should adjust placement settings, adjust creative for specific placements, or accept what Meta is telling them about where the audience actually lives.

## What never to do

- Never recommend forcing budget to a specific placement just because the brand wants that audience there. The algorithm is reading the creative — fix the creative, not the placement settings.
- Never ignore the breakdown effect when reading placement distribution.
- Never treat CPM in isolation. CPM divided by Cost per 1,000 Accounts Reached tells the reach efficiency story.
