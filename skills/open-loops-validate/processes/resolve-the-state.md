# Resolve the state

After the plan has run and the reverse-evidence is in, the loop resolves into exactly one of four states. The decision is measured against the bar the hypothesis set before any research ran — not a bar adjusted to fit what came back.

## The four states

**Validated.** The research confirms the prediction with sufficient evidence, and the evidence for it clearly outweighs the evidence against. "Sufficient" is the bar the hypothesis set — the named recurrence, spread, and recency. The brand gets a new confirmed insight; the closure doc goes to the user and the insight is added to the brand profile with its verbatim evidence and its date.

**Invalidated.** The research returns a clear no — the evidence against the prediction outweighs the evidence for it, or the prediction simply does not hold up. This is a real result, not a failure. The brand profile gains a known dead-end so the question is not re-litigated, the user is told what was found and why it was a no, and the document is archived under `invalidated`.

**Inconclusive.** The research found strong, roughly equal evidence both ways, or the question turned out more complex than the prediction allowed. The honest read is that the evidence does not settle it. Either propose re-running the validation a different way Parker believes will resolve it, or archive it with a plain account of how complex it was, so a similar loop later inherits the reasoning.

**Insufficient evidence.** The research could not find enough data to answer either way. This is genuinely too little or missing data — distinct from inconclusive, where there was plenty of evidence but it pointed both ways. State the gap plainly and store the document so a future pass with better data can pick it up.

## The line that gets blurred

The two soft states get confused, and the difference matters:

- **Inconclusive = enough evidence, no clear direction.** Plenty came back; it pointed both ways. The question is harder than the prediction assumed.
- **Insufficient = not enough evidence to judge.** Little or nothing came back. The sources were thin, blocked, or silent.

Naming which one it actually is tells the next pass what to do differently: inconclusive needs a sharper question or a different cut; insufficient needs better data or a source Parker could not reach this time.

## The discipline

- **Measure against the pre-set bar.** If the hypothesis said three independent sources at notable recurrence would validate it, and two came back, it is not validated because the finding was interesting. The bar was set honestly in advance; hold to it.
- **Weigh the reverse-evidence.** The state reflects the balance of for and against. A pile of confirming evidence with a real body of contradicting evidence is inconclusive, not validated.
- **Resolve to one state.** If the loop genuinely split into two questions that resolved differently, that is a sign it was two loops; note it and route the second back to the roll-up rather than forcing one state onto both.
- **The verdict carries its reasoning.** Whatever the state, the closure doc says why — what the evidence showed, how the two sides weighed, and why that lands here and not in an adjacent state.
