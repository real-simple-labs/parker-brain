# Process — Speed and Transformation Hooks

Combine time-based urgency with visible before-and-after transformation proof. Shows fast results and tangible change to create instant gratification while building trust through visual proof.

## Psychological framework

- **Instant gratification** — people want fast, visible results.
- **Proof through transformation** — seeing is believing.
- **Urgency** — time elements drive immediate interest.
- **Fear of missing out** — quick results means do not delay.

## Required inputs

- A real, verifiable transformation that can be shown visually. Before-and-after footage, timeline footage, or visible result documentation.
- The actual timeframe for the transformation. Realistic numbers only — overpromising backfires.
- Compliance check — health, weight, and medical categories have strict before-and-after rules.

## When to pick

- **Best for:** beauty and cosmetics. Skincare. Fitness and wellness. Any product with visible, fast, undeniable change. Products that solve urgent problems.
- **Worst for:** products with slow or invisible benefits. Categories where before-and-after claims violate compliance. Brands where the transformation cannot be authentically shown.

## Execution

1. **Lock the timeframe.** "7 days," "overnight," "within minutes" — specific numbers. Vague timing weakens the hook.
2. **Show the transformation visually.** Before-and-after split screen, time-lapse, or progressive shots. Visual proof is critical — not just talking about it.
3. **Pair specificity with proof.** "7 days to clear skin" with day 1 → day 7 visual is far stronger than "see results fast."
4. **Be realistic.** Overpromising on timeframe backfires when reality lands.
5. **Direct the visual.** Text overlay with the timeframe and outcome. Split screen or progressive transformation. Timer counting up or down. Creator's face throughout for human connection.

## Output content

- The exact text overlay with timeframe and outcome.
- The visual sequence with timing.
- The ICP and the transformation desire the hook is targeting.
- The proven-pattern note.
- Compliance flag — especially for health, weight, and medical claims.

## What never to do

- Use a vague timeframe.
- Make a before-and-after claim that cannot be verified or violates platform compliance.
- Use this hook on a product without genuine fast transformation.
- Overpromise. The audience can detect inflation.
