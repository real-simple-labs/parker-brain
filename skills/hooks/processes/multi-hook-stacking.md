# Process — Multi-Hook Stacking

Stack two or three hooks at the beginning of the ad instead of relying on one. Creates a "hook reel" that prevents drop-off and appeals to multiple psychological triggers simultaneously.

The stacked hooks must be DIFFERENT formats — stacking three comment-response hooks back-to-back is not stacking. Demographic + controversy + comment-response is.

## Psychological framework

- **Sustained engagement** — prevents drop-off in the first few seconds.
- **Multi-trigger** — appeals to different psychological motivators.
- **Pattern variation** — keeps the brain engaged through change.
- **Reinforcement** — multiple chances to hook the viewer.

## Required inputs

- An existing winning ad or strong concept to stack hooks onto. This process is mostly for refreshing existing content, not for net-new from scratch.
- Brand context to pick complementary hook formats.
- An understanding of what hook formats the brand has not yet exhausted.

## When to pick

- **Best for:** preventing early drop-off on existing concepts. Complex value propositions. Audiences with varied motivations. Improving existing high-performers. Testing efficiency — one video, multiple entry points. Particularly effective for 40+ and women-skewed audiences.
- **Worst for:** brand-new concepts with no existing body to stack onto. Premium brands where rapid-fire openings read as off-brand. Audiences with very short attention where three hooks is too much before the body.

## Execution

1. **Pick two or three complementary hook formats.** Different formats — demographic + controversy, demographic + comment-response, scam + investment, POV + question. Each stacked hook activates a different psychological lever.
2. **Each mini-hook is punchy and quick.** 2-3 seconds per hook. The full stack is 6-9 seconds before the body begins.
3. **Use visual elements between hooks.** Drip content, explosion clips, abstract art, fruit explosions, color shifts. The visual element marks the transition between hooks and adds re-engagement.
4. **Order matters.** Open with the strongest qualifying hook (often demographic), then go to the highest-tension hook (controversy, scam, comment-response), then the cleanest emotional entry (POV, question, conversational).
5. **Maximum three hooks.** More becomes too much.

## Output content

- The three hook formats stacked, in order, with rationale for each.
- The exact content of each mini-hook — text overlay, line, visual.
- The visual transition between hooks.
- The transition into the existing body.
- The ICP and the multi-trigger strategy.
- The proven-pattern note.

## What never to do

- Stack the same format multiple times. The point is variety.
- Skip the visual transition between hooks. Hard cuts without a visual marker feel disjointed.
- More than three hooks. Diminishing returns and audience drop-off.
- Stack onto a body that does not match the energy of the hooks.
