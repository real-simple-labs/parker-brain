# Process — Conversational / Native Format Hooks

Hooks that do not even feel like hooks. Casual, mid-conversation phrases that make viewers feel like they are jumping into a conversation already happening.

## Psychological framework

- **Pattern interrupt** — does not signal "ad" to the brain.
- **Conversational priming** — feels like talking to a friend.
- **Curiosity gap** — jumping mid-conversation makes you want context.
- **Natural attention** — how we actually talk in real life.
- **Reduced resistance** — lower psychological barriers than traditional ads.

## Required inputs

- A natural mid-conversation opener that fits the brand voice. "No because...," "Okay these...," "Wait why is this...," "Okay so..."
- A creator with authentic, natural on-camera presence. This hook fails on stilted delivery.
- Brand voice — casual register has to be on-brand.

## When to pick

- **Best for:** making ads feel native and organic. All creator content from B2B to beauty to apparel. Building authenticity. Reducing ad fatigue. Gen Z and Millennial audiences. Both paid and organic.
- **Worst for:** premium brands where casual conversational register reads as off-brand. Categories where the audience expects a more formal pitch.

## Execution

1. **Pick the conversational opener.** "No because these are actually insane," "Okay these are so good," "Wait why is no one talking about this," "Okay so I just discovered..."
2. **Do not announce the ad or product immediately.** The conversational entry has to feel like the conversation was already happening.
3. **Maintain casual tone throughout.** The conversational opener has to flow into a casual delivery of the rest of the ad. If the body shifts to formal pitch, the conversational hook is wasted.
4. **Lock the visual.** Creator mid-gesture, looking off camera or directly at it, casual setting. Natural hand movements, no formal introduction.

## Output content

- The exact conversational opener.
- The transition into the rest of the ad while maintaining casual tone.
- The creator brief — authentic, natural, casual.
- The ICP and the native feel being targeted.
- The proven-pattern note.

## What never to do

- Use this hook on a brand where the voice does not support casual register.
- Open with the product or the offer. The conversational entry must hold for a beat.
- Use this with a creator who reads as scripted or stiff. Authentic delivery is non-negotiable.
- Shift to formal pitch in the body. The whole ad has to feel conversational.
