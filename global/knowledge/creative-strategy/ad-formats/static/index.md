# Static ad formats

Source: `AI Tagging - Ad Format.csv`, added 2026-06-04.

These are the formats that are primarily layout-driven, screenshot-like, headline-led, product-arrangement-led, native-photo-led, or designed as a single visual composition. They can stack with tags from `both/index.md` when the ad earns both.

## Static format tags

### Billboard

The ad replicates a real or fake billboard, bus stop ad, subway ad, Times Square placement, public signage, or another outdoor advertising environment. The public placement is visible and central to the concept.

### Headline + Benefits

A clear non-question, non-offer headline is paired with a scannable list of product benefits. The benefits may appear as bullets, numbers, checkmarks, short rows, or other quick-read elements.

### Carousel

A multi-frame ad unit built for horizontal swiping, user-directed pacing, and modular information. It usually uses arrows, pagination, dots, progress cues, or a sequence of connected cards. The format is the multi-frame architecture itself.

### Handwritten

Actual or realistic handwriting is the main creative element. The ad looks like a personal written note on paper, a notepad, or another physical writing surface.

### Atypical Text

Text appears in an unusual real-world location — a foggy mirror, skin, sand, sidewalk, condensation, objects, projection, or another surface where ad copy is unexpected. The novelty of where the text appears is the concept.

### Headline Only

The static has one dominant headline with no meaningful supporting benefit list or body copy. It may include a product image, logo, small badge, or minor callout, but the headline carries the message.

### Product Collection

Multiple products, a kit, a bundle, or a curated set dominate the visual. The products themselves are the star, and text supports the display rather than leading the concept.

### Meme

The ad uses a recognizable meme template, viral internet image, or known meme format, with the product or brand message inserted into that familiar structure.

### Native

The static looks intentionally like an organic, unedited photo rather than an ad. There is no headline, graphic overlay, designed hierarchy, or obvious sales layer. It often resembles a camera-roll photo or native content unit.

### Notes App

The ad mimics the Apple Notes app interface, including the recognizable note layout, system typography, and native app feel. The content can be a confession, list, note to self, recommendation, or another written message.

### Press

The ad mimics a press article, publication feature, editorial list, or media screenshot. It borrows visual trust from outlets, article layouts, publication logos, or editorial design.

### Text Message

The ad mimics a smartphone text conversation, usually iMessage-style blue and gray bubbles. The conversation naturally leads to or mentions the product.

### Us vs Them

A static side-by-side comparison between the brand and a competitor, category alternative, or old behavior. The layout explicitly shows two sides and contrasts attributes, benefits, drawbacks, checks, X marks, or similar comparison signals. For a video where the comparison unfolds through time, see `Comparison` in `video/index.md`.

### Question-based Headline

The most prominent text is a question designed to trigger self-recognition, curiosity, or reflection. The question is the visual hierarchy leader.
