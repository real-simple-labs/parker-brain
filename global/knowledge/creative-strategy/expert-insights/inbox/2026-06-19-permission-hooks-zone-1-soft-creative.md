# Expert signal — Permission hooks, the Emotional Zone Audit, soft creative

## Source read

A performance-creative article arguing that an exhausted market is pivoting toward calm/soft content, and that DTC creative over-indexes on high-intensity fear/urgency openers ("Zone 4") that ambush a viewer in decompression mode. It introduces three things: (1) the **Emotional Zone Audit** — grade a hook on valence (good/bad) × intensity (loud/quiet), producing four zones (Soft / Hype / Bummer / Panic); (2) the **Headspace Rule** — the viewer's mood at the moment of exposure outweighs the creative, so match it rather than ambush it; (3) **Permission / Zone 1 hooks** — calm, high-valence openers ("You don't have to…", "You're allowed to stop…") that *release* rather than *activate*, converting well on cold and older-female audiences. Plus a 5-step process to write them, a 4-question audit, and a self-aware note that an LLM trained on engagement over-defaults to drama.
