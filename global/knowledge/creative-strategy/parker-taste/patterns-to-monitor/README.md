# Creative Strategy Patterns To Monitor

This is Parker's watch list: emerging creative-strategy patterns Parker has seen named but has not promoted into taste, method, or a brand idea bank. A pattern lives here when it is plausible and worth tracking, but the evidence is too thin to act on — usually a single expert source, an account-state-dependent claim, or a hypothesis Parker has not yet seen in real account data.

## What a pattern entry must do

The job of an entry is to teach the pattern, not to name it. A bullet that says "persona-specific asset remixing" tells Parker nothing it could act on. An entry has to extract enough that Parker could recognize the pattern in a real account and reason about whether it is happening. Every entry carries, in narrative prose:

- **The pattern** — what it actually is, described concretely enough to picture, not as a label.
- **Why it would work** — the mechanism. The reasoning a strategist would give for why this should move performance.
- **What it looks like in the wild** — the tactical recognition layer. The signals, the worked numbers, the concrete examples from the source that let Parker spot the pattern or run the calculation, rather than just believe it exists.
- **Why it is still a watch item** — the evidence gap. What is missing before this can be trusted: corroboration, account data, or the fact that it is account-state-dependent.
- **What would promote it, and where it goes** — the condition that moves it off the watch list, and which surface it would update when it does: a context doc, a knowledge doc, a process, a prompt, or the idea bank.

Keep the expert claim separate from Parker inference. A pattern here is what someone said is working, sharpened so Parker understands it — not something Parker has confirmed.

## What does not belong here

A pattern that Parker has already adopted is not a watch item — it is method, and it lives in the relevant context doc, skill, or system doc. When a source mostly validates Parker's existing direction, the right move is to note that it reinforced an existing surface, not to park it here as if it were still open. The watch list is for what Parker has not yet decided, not for what Parker already does.

## Promotion and review

A pattern is promoted when a second independent source corroborates it, Parker sees it in account data, or Jimmy approves it. On promotion, the learning moves into the surface that owns it and the watch entry is retired. Patterns are revisited during monthly expert-signal curation and the quarterly Parker-taste update. A pattern that has sat unconfirmed for several cycles should be reconsidered rather than left to imply it is gaining weight.
