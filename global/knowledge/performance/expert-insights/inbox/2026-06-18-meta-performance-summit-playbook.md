## Expert claim

The connected claims, in the operator's framing:

- **Reels creative essentials (3 rules).** (1) Stay in the *safe zone* — keep text/logos out of the UI strips; it's now a delivery filter, not a formatting tip, and breaking it is penalized in the auction with higher CPMs. (2) Full-screen 9x16 vertical video only — not 1x1 or 4x5; claimed ~35% lower CPA than image ads on reels. (3) Design for *sound-on* — voiceover, creator dialogue, music synced to cuts; sound is a core delivery signal; stock music doesn't count.
- **Reels are content, not commercials.** Every ad is just organic content you pay to distribute. If it looks like an ad ("an export from Final Cut"), it won't be watched. The three properties: relatable, digestible, entertaining. Creator-made reels beat product-focused reels on view-through, shares/saves, and CPA across verticals.
- **Partnership ads are the best inventory.** Run the ad from the creator's handle with your budget/targeting/CTA. Claimed ~19% lower CPA, ~53% higher CTR; adding partnership ads to more placements improves CPC/CTR/through-play. Mechanism: Meta uses both your signals and the creator's page signals to rank/deliver; a creator with *low* audience overlap reaches genuinely new people. Meta is "putting its thumb on the scale" (cheaper CPMs) to drive adoption — a temporary edge expected to fade by ~Q4. Use the Instagram creator marketplace; the speaker plugs insense.pro as a managed option.
- **Creator-brief rules (3).** (1) Tell them *why them* — what about their voice/content fits. (2) Let them tell the story — brief an idea/format ("what's in/what's out," GRWM), not a 15-bullet script. (3) Don't over-brand — guard rails, not handcuffs; creators ≠ influencers (hire for the content, not necessarily the audience).
- **Incrementality is the north star.** Incrementality measures what *actually* worked, not what got credit. Last-click ROAS over-credits sales that would have happened anyway. Claimed: 35% of spend wasted on non-incremental sales under last-click; 78% of advertisers still use last-click; on a "$740B dataset" that's ">$200B wasted." The "Pedro vs John" frame: Pedro buys anyway (always clicks); John buys *because* of the ad — pay to reach Johns. Gen Z is ~2x more likely to buy without clicking; ~60% of FB/IG time is video → view attribution over click.
- **Run conversion-lift / holdout tests.** Hold out ~20% as control, show ads to 80%, measure the delta = true incremental lift. Available free in Ads Manager (Experiments → conversion lift / incremental attribution). Meta's stated guidance (for million-a-month brands): ~$50k per cell, ~50 conversions/week for significance — but the speaker insists smaller brands can still run it. The reframe: *incremental attribution is just optimizing for new customers.*
- **Calibrate, don't worship one number.** After a lift test, read the delta (test purchases − control purchases). Use it to calibrate MTA/MMM: if MTA said Meta drove 100 and lift says 150, apply a 1.5x multiplier. Cost-controls (cost/bid caps) usually turn out ~10–15% incremental — the rest is credit-taking.
- **The three-layer measurement stack.** Layer 1 platform attribution (fast/cheap, daily pacing). Layer 2 MTA/MMM (cross-channel, budget splits). Layer 3 lift tests (gold standard, calibrates the other two). Align all three; don't pick a favorite (the "favorite" usually just flatters the highest-paid person in the room).
- **Measurement rhythm.** Weekly: platform + MTA feedback for pacing. Monthly: spot-check model drift (are platform conversions diverging from modeled?). Quarterly: run a lift, calibrate, reallocate. Respect seasonality / year-over-year.
- **Account structure: simple scales, complex fails.** Advantage+ is now a *state* a campaign is in, not a campaign type. Feed the machine clean signals and get out of the way; you're the manager of the factory, not the laborer. Over-segmentation (many ad sets, manual placements, audience split-tests, constant tinkering) starves the system of statistical confidence. 8-figure accounts look "boring": a hero campaign, a catalog, a retargeting loop; clean naming, broad audiences, stable budgets.
- **Signal quality is the real lever.** EMQ (event match quality) should be 8+; below that, invest in CAPI to match browser events. Send both browser (pixel) and server (CAPI) events — Meta dedups. Build *custom conversion events* for what matters to you (new customer, hero SKU) so the machine optimizes toward it. Use value optimization to optimize for *profit*, not just AOV, by passing real margin per item. Watch catalog match rate (a low one means DPA is missing half its possible sales). Assign an internal signal owner; review weekly.
- **AI = execution, not strategy.** Meta's AI can write headlines, build creatives, adjust budgets — but it can't know your margins, LTV, offer, or which segment is worth more. You still own three levers: the offer, the creative, and signal quality. "Facebook is a volume knob on the rest of your business."
- **Performance scorecard + opportunity score.** Diagnostics, not guarantees. The scorecard grades best-practice adoption (Advantage+ state %, Advantage+ audience/budget/placements %, reels creative essentials, CAPI coverage, EMQ, catalog match rate). The opportunity score (0–100, "like a credit score") gives proven/personalized/prioritized recommendations that update as more advertisers act on them. Treat ~80/20: a B+ with more headroom beats chasing a perfect score.
