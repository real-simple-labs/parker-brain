## Expert claim

The connected claims, in the operators' own framing:

- **Grade an ad by the funnel job it does — the percent-new-visits / CPMR instrumentation of "optimize for new customers."** An ad driving 85–90% new visits is held to a *lower* ROAS bar than one at ~50%, because the two do different funnel jobs. Shutting off high-new-visit ads on a one-day-click metric kills the prospecting that does not convert same-day. This is the same north star the Disruptor seed names — incremental attribution is optimizing for new customers — but instrumented as a concrete ad-grading rule a media buyer applies in the account. Stated as validated with House (causal) brand-lift tests showing moves that hurt one-day-click were more incremental.
- **"New visits" ≠ "net-new reach."** An ad can drive ~95% new *visits* while reaching almost no net-new *people* — it re-reaches a warm audience in a new way that finally gets the click (their "Joe Rogan clip" example). The marketer's job is new *visitors and interest*, achievable without net-new reach. Sharpens how a percent-new-visits read is used so it isn't mistaken for a reach metric.
- **CPMR as a demand-vs-harvest health metric.** Spending more to reach fewer people degrades performance and is felt downstream even when current numbers look fine (analogous to over-harvesting existing customers). They track cost per thousand *reached* weekly/monthly as a directional warning (rising = CPMs up or frequency up). Meta's rolling-reach window (~18–24 months) is the wrong frame; weekly/monthly rolling reach is more useful and achievable.
- **Brand lift is a new, un-benchmarked KPI for retail/Amazon expansion.** As more demand is captured on Amazon and retail with non-branded search, the job becomes driving recognition off-platform. Near-term they only try to drive down cost-per-brand-lift; they openly do not yet know how to value a lift or attach it to latent revenue, and won't scale into it until a long-horizon House/geo study ties it to downstream revenue. (A lift-test posture that corroborates the seed's conversion-lift method, with the valuation question left open.)
- **Refinement on "simplify, give Meta everything": physical-goods ecom needs guardrails the pure-incrementality posture doesn't provide.** Meta wants unfiltered data and full liquidity, but a brand with price-segmented categories, inventory it has paid for, and new lines it must invest ahead of demand for **cannot** just optimize for the next incremental dollar today. It must force-spend specific SKUs and build lines for a year out. Current workarounds (signal-shaping, adset min/max) feel hacky; the operators want campaign-level "do your thing, but here are the must-move guardrails." This is a genuine *tension* with the seed's "simple scales, complex fails / give the machine clean signals and get out of the way" posture — both can be true, and the refinement is that physical-goods inventory and ahead-of-demand investment are context the incrementality machine cannot see.
- **CASK / combined awareness + sales — compelling in theory, unproven in practice.** The hybrid reach + purchase-optimized type theoretically uses GEM/sequence-learning to reach people who'll be in-market in ~3 months and to flex liquidity between reach and conversion by demand. One host loves the theory; both are skeptical in practice and testing it.
- **Catalog enrichment + GMV-maxification + the creator flywheel are Meta's direction, copied from TikTok.** Old catalog (DPA) "never worked"; the new enriched SKU-level catalog + copy + business-goal signals is the substrate Meta generates creative from. Meta shipped product tagging as a first step toward a native creator flywheel; affiliates posting and tagging products are reframed as a **form of catalog enrichment**; "much less brand-internal creative" ahead. (Corroborates the seed's Advantage+-as-a-state / signal-quality direction from the demand side.)
- **TikTok Shop is mostly paid, not organic-dependent — and catalog activation surfaces unexpected winners.** Brands sitting on TikTok Shop mostly promote creator content via GMV Max (i.e. it's ads). The unlock is volume: flood the zone (one host cites 1,200 affiliate posts/week), overpay short-term, whittle to winners. Let affiliates activate the **entire** catalog, not just hero SKUs — mid/long-tail products find angles that wouldn't pencil elsewhere. Creator concentration is brutal (an unnamed top-10 brand: "6 creators drive 60% of revenue"). Tooling: Yuka; creator deals as retainer vs %-of-spend vs %-of-sales; underwriting at ~10x content efficiency.
