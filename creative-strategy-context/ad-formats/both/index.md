# Cross-modality ad formats

Source: `AI Tagging - Ad Format.csv`, added 2026-06-04.

These formats can be executed as video or static. They are defined by the creative device, source of credibility, interaction pattern, or message structure rather than by motion alone.

## Video or static format tags

### Aesthetic

The ad intentionally makes the product feel premium, elevated, refined, curated, or luxury-coded. This is about deliberate styling, not camera quality — a phone-shot ad can be aesthetic if the lighting, set, styling, and composition are elevated. This is distinct from `High Production`, which is about commercial-grade filming.

### Authority Figure

The ad centers on a credible expert, specialist, professional, or domain authority whose credentials make the endorsement or explanation carry weight. The authority can be signaled through title, spoken intro, setting, wardrobe, lower third, or professional context.

### Before & After

The ad opens with a clear visual comparison between a before state and an after state. The comparison appears in or near the hook and creates an immediate contrast.

### Celebrity

The ad features a widely recognized public figure — someone famous enough that the audience is expected to know them without explanation. When the person needs their credentials explained, it is `Authority Figure`.

### Social Interface

The creative prominently uses a recognizable platform interface or UI element — Reddit, Instagram, Twitter/X, Facebook, Google Search, ChatGPT, a comment bubble, a tweet, or a search result page. The interface is part of the creative itself.

### Comment Response

The ad is built around responding to a specific comment, review, or piece of user feedback that is visually displayed or clearly referenced. The response is the concept, not just a proof point.

### Educational

The ad teaches the viewer something new — usually the why behind a problem, a misconception, a mechanism, a category truth, or product relevance. The product often appears after the insight has reframed the problem. The viewer should genuinely learn something.

### Employee Generated Content

The ad features actual brand employees or clearly employee-coded people creating or appearing in the creative. It often feels behind-the-scenes, insider, office, warehouse, product-development, or team-led.

### Founder Ads

The founder or co-founder is the primary figure, and the ad revolves around their story, reason for building the company, product belief, or direct endorsement.

### How To

The ad uses an instructional structure that teaches the viewer how to do something. The hook makes the instructional promise clear, and the ad delivers a process or steps.

### Humour

The ad deliberately uses jokes, absurdity, sarcasm, irony, controversy, unhinged behavior, or comic contrast to entertain. The goal is amusement alongside or instead of straightforward selling.

### Listicle

The hook promises a specific number of reasons, tips, arguments, or persuasive points, and the ad delivers the list. The list structure is the format.

### News

The creative looks and feels like a news broadcast — a broadcaster, breaking-news style, lower thirds, tickers, live indicators, studio cues, or similar news visual language. The news style appears in the hook or main frame.

### Offer Based

The ad leads with a promotional offer, discount, BOGO, free gift, free shipping threshold, sale, or other financial incentive as the primary, dominant concept.

### Post-It Note

A physical post-it note is the primary visual device. The note may be placed on a product, mirror, wall, desk, skin, package, or other relevant surface.

### POV

The text `POV` or `POV:` appears on screen, followed by a situational statement. The literal text cue is the defining feature.

### Warehouse

The ad is visually set in the brand's warehouse, fulfillment center, factory, or shipping environment. The behind-the-scenes operational setting defines the format.

### Callout

The hook directly addresses a specific demographic, audience segment, identity, life stage, or situation. It speaks to the viewer with second-person or direct-address language.

### Interactive

The ad looks like something the viewer can tap, select, swipe, choose, answer, or interact with, even though the actual creative is not interactive. It uses buttons, quizzes, decision paths, tappable-looking elements, or engagement-bait UI.

### Other

The format that applies when no defined format can be confidently assigned.
