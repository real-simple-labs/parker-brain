# Creative Consumption Analysis

Understanding the content a persona **already consumes organically** in their own feed — so the ads you make for them blend in to stand out. Distinct from general organic analysis: this is run *per persona*, anchored to their psychographics, and its output is a production-style brief, not a trend report.

The logic: an ad that looks and feels like the content a persona already chooses to watch slips past the "this is an ad" filter. To do that, you have to know what that content actually looks like for *this* persona.

## What you're trying to learn

For a given persona, characterise the organic content they live in:

- **Creator type** — who's on camera? Age, look, perceived expertise, peer vs. authority.
- **Production level** — raw and handheld, or polished and lit? Ring light or natural? Cuts or one-take?
- **Setting** — bathroom, kitchen, car, makeup chair, outdoors? The recurring backdrops.
- **Delivery** — straight-to-camera and confessional, voiceover-over-b-roll, demonstration, skit?
- **Tone & pacing** — fast and punchy, or slow and authentic?
- **Recurring formats** — the format patterns that dominate the niche organically.

You're reverse-engineering the native aesthetic so production can match it deliberately rather than defaulting to a generic ad look.

## The method

### 1. Reverse-engineer the search terms

The persona's organic feed is reachable through the terms they'd search. Derive those terms from their fears, frustrations, and desires (the 5 Fs) — the language they use about the problem, not your product language.

> Ask Parker or Claude: *"Based on this persona's fears and desires, give me the search terms they'd type into TikTok / YouTube to find content about this problem."*

e.g. a mature-skin makeup persona → *"best foundation for over 60 skin."* These are pre-purchase, problem-led searches — exactly what the persona does before they ever see your ad.

### 2. Search and observe

Run the terms in TikTok / YouTube and watch what surfaces — especially what's performing organically (views, likes, comments). Note the recurring pattern across the top results against the checklist above. Usually a clear theme emerges fast (e.g. "a woman, in her bathroom, talking straight to camera, no cuts, natural-ish light").

### 3. Capture and summarise

Pull the strongest reference links into the persona's doc under a **Creative Consumption Analysis** section, with a short written summary of the common themes. Optionally feed the references to an AI to extract shared patterns — *"here are 10 organic videos that did well; spot the common themes for a persona analysis"* — but a manual pass catches nuance the tools miss.

## Why it's its own analysis

- It's **pre-purchase and organic** — a different lens from reviews/surveys (post-purchase) and from competitor ads (paid). It tells you how the persona wants to be *spoken to*, not just what they want.
- It's **per persona** — different personas in the same brand consume visibly different content; a single brand-level "organic trends" pass averages that away.
- Its output is a **production directive** — it directly shapes the brief: who to cast, where to shoot, how raw, what format.

## Where this gets used

- **Persona deep dives** — a standard section of the deep persona analysis, completed per persona.
- **Briefs & production** — feeds casting, setting, and production-level decisions so the ad is format-native to the persona's feed.
- **Format selection** — confirms which ad formats are native to the persona before scripting.
