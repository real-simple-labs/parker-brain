# New SKU Launch

Launching a new SKU into an existing catalog — a new colorway, flavor, scent, fabric, size, format, bundle, or spinoff for an **established brand** — is not a separate method from a new-product launch. It is the same method, worked at the depth-play and bridge-and-expand end of it.

**The full playbook lives in [new-product-launches.md](new-product-launches.md).** Read it whenever you get a new-SKU brief. In that doc's terms, most new-SKU launches land in:

- **Scenario A — Depth Play:** same product, new attribute (color, flavor, scent, fabric, size) or a collection-drop wrapper. Inherit the winning structures from the closest existing SKU and make the newness visible.
- **Scenario B — Bridge & Expand:** same product family, new format / occasion / use case / bundle. Anchor in the hero product's authority and introduce the new format as a natural extension.

The classifier (Step Zero), diagnostic (Step 1), awareness-stage filter, proof mapping, and output specs are all in that doc. A new SKU that targets a genuinely new audience is Scenario C there, and a brand launching its very first product is out of scope for the launch method entirely.

*This is based on everything I have learned about launching ads for new products and SKUs.*
