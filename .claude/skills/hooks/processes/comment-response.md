# Process — Comment Response Hooks

Open with a TikTok-style comment overlay and respond to it directly in the video. The creator must address the comment in conversation — not just have it overlaid with no relation to the dialogue.

## Psychological framework

- **Pattern interrupt** — reads as organic TikTok content, not as an ad.
- **Social proof** — implies other people are asking these questions too.
- **Trust building** — addressing objections head-on reduces perceived risk.
- **Authenticity signal** — real comments imply real people care about this product.

## Required inputs

- A real customer objection or comment pulled from reviews, post-purchase surveys, or ad comments. Made-up comments fail the authenticity check.
- The brand's response position on that objection.
- Brand voice and ICP.

## When to pick

- **Best for:** products with common, named objections. Categories where skepticism is the dominant emotion. Retargeting campaigns where specific hesitations need addressing. Skeptical or controversial comments often outperform neutral ones.
- **Worst for:** brands without real review or comment volume to draw from. Categories where the audience is enthusiastic rather than skeptical. Premium brands where reading comments would feel beneath the brand.

## Execution

1. **Source the real comment.** Pull from reviews, post-purchase surveys, ad comments, or DMs. Verbatim if possible. If the comment needs lightly cleaned wording, preserve the voice — never sanitize it into marketing language.
2. **Position the response.** The creator must directly address what the comment said. Not generic enthusiasm — actual engagement with the specific objection or claim.
3. **Lock the visual.** Creator on camera, casual setting, comment overlay near the top of the frame. Slow zoom and minor jump cuts keep the energy organic without reading as edited.
4. **Write the line.** The opening line must explicitly acknowledge the comment — "Oh yeah, I cannot stand stevia" engaging the comment "I cannot stand stevia" — then transitions naturally into the product benefits or counter-argument.

## Output content

- The exact comment overlay text.
- The creator's opening spoken line that responds to it.
- The transition from response into the rest of the ad body.
- The ICP and emotion this comment targets.
- The proven-pattern note tied to the comment-response framework.

## What never to do

- Fabricate a comment. The source has to be real even if lightly cleaned.
- Use a comment that does not connect to a real objection in the brand's audience.
- Have the comment overlaid without the creator actually engaging it.
- Pull from the format's source examples in the library — those are for pattern recognition, not for output.
