# Process — Controversy / Anti-Traditional Hooks

Go against the grain, break a tradition, or introduce debate. Identifies a common enemy, hot take, or widely-held belief and challenges it directly to force engagement.

## Psychological framework

- **Polarization power** — debate forces engagement.
- **Pattern disruption** — challenges expectations.
- **Tribal alignment** — creates us vs them.
- **Curiosity** — must hear the argument.
- **Engagement boost** — agreement and disagreement both drive comments.

## Required inputs

- A genuinely contested practice, belief, or industry norm in the brand's category.
- A legitimate counter-argument the brand can defend. Manufactured controversy without substance backfires.
- Brand voice — controversial language has a register that not all brands can carry.

## When to pick

- **Best for:** products that challenge category norms. Crowded markets. Differentiation from competitors. Driving engagement and comments. Products with unique mechanisms. Categories with established "wrong" solutions. Sophisticated markets where people have tried many things.
- **Worst for:** brands where the audience expects a softer voice. Categories without genuine norms to push against. Brands without a defensible counter-argument.

## Execution

1. **Name the target practice or belief.** Specific. "STOP using retinol once a week" works; "the old way is wrong" does not.
2. **Pick the trigger phrasing.** "STOP doing X," "NEVER do Y," "If you're doing Z you're f*cked," "[Practice] is killing your [outcome]."
3. **Promise the alternative.** The controversy creates the curiosity; the resolution provides the alternative method the brand offers.
4. **Lock the visual.** Demonstrate the wrong way visually with a red X or strike-through, then reveal the right way with a check mark or product reveal.
5. **Accept that some viewers will disagree.** Engagement from disagreement is still engagement — both drive algorithmic distribution.

## Output content

- The exact controversial claim or trigger statement.
- The visual rejection of the old way.
- The reveal of the alternative.
- The proof or argument backing the counter-claim.
- The proven-pattern note.

## What never to do

- Manufacture controversy without substance. The argument must be real.
- Pick a target the brand cannot defend.
- Use controversial language on brands where the voice does not support it.
- Leave the controversy without a resolution — the alternative must be the brand's offer.
- Attack a target so beloved that the controversy alienates rather than engages.
