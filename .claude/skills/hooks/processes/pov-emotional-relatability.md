# Process — POV / Emotional Relatability Hooks

Use "POV" (point of view) or "I hate / I love" framing to put the viewer directly in a specific scenario or mindset, often paired with strong emotional trigger words.

## Psychological framework

- **Empathy activation** — puts the viewer directly in the scenario.
- **Tribal identity** — creates in-group feeling.
- **Emotional resonance** — strong trigger words amplify effect.
- **Native format** — feels like organic social content, not an ad.

## Required inputs

- A highly specific, highly relatable scenario the audience experiences. Generic scenarios fail.
- The emotion the scenario activates — frustration, joy, recognition, rage.
- Brand voice — POV language has a casual register that needs to fit the brand.

## When to pick

- **Best for:** highly relatable everyday situations. Lifestyle and identity-based products. Gen Z and Millennial audiences. Partnership ads with creators. Appears in 10-15% of top-performing creative.
- **Worst for:** premium brands where casual language reads as off-brand. Categories without highly specific relatable scenarios. Older audiences who do not engage with POV format.

## Execution

1. **Lock the specific scenario.** "POV: you hate when your skincare pills up under makeup" works. "POV: you don't like your skin" does not. Specificity is everything.
2. **Pair with a strong emotional word.** "Hate," "love," "obsessed," "tired of," "done with" — strong feelings outperform mild ones.
3. **Match the visual to the scenario.** Show the situation playing out. Exaggerated reactions are okay — preferred, even.
4. **Direct the transition.** From the relatable scenario into the product as the response. The product enters as the resolution to the POV.

## Output content

- The exact POV line (or "I hate/love" alternative).
- The scenario being staged.
- The exaggerated visual reaction.
- The transition from POV into product or solution.
- The ICP and emotion the POV is activating.
- The proven-pattern note.

## What never to do

- Generic POV scenarios that could apply to anyone.
- POV format on brands where the voice does not support casual register.
- Skip the strong emotional word. POV without emotion is just description.
- Hold the POV without resolving into the product.
