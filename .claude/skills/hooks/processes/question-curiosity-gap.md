# Process — Question / Curiosity Gap Hooks

Open with a direct question the viewer instinctively wants answered. Can be rhetorical, provocative, or genuinely seeking engagement. The brain seeks closure to unanswered questions — that drive is the lever.

## Psychological framework

- **Open loop** — brain seeks closure to questions.
- **Personal relevance** — questions force self-assessment.
- **Engagement** — viewers mentally or actually answer.
- **Curiosity activation** — must know the answer.
- **Conversational signal** — questions feel like a conversation, not a pitch.

## Required inputs

- A question that matches the audience's actual pain, desire, or curiosity. Generic questions fail.
- The answer the ad will provide. Questions must be answered — leaving them hanging breaks trust.
- Brand voice.

## When to pick

- **Best for:** engaging curiosity. Problem-aware audiences. Educational content. Sparking debate. Interactive-feeling ads. Solution-aware audiences considering options.
- **Worst for:** unaware audiences who do not have the question yet. Categories where rhetorical questions feel manipulative.

## Execution

1. **Lock the question.** Tied directly to a pain or desire the audience has. "Is it possible to get clear skin without giving up dairy?" works. "Do you want healthier skin?" does not.
2. **Pause after the question.** The visual or text holds for a beat to let the viewer mentally answer.
3. **Answer the question.** The ad must deliver on the question. The answer is the product or the insight.
4. **Visual support.** Thoughtful expression, pause, then transition to the answer with demonstration or proof.

## Output content

- The exact question text/voiceover.
- The pause and visual.
- The transition into the answer.
- The ICP and the curiosity being activated.
- The proven-pattern note.

## What never to do

- Use questions with obvious yes/no answers. "Do you want to look younger?" wastes the format.
- Leave the question unanswered. Always deliver.
- Generic questions that any audience would answer the same way.
- Use this hook on unaware audiences who do not yet have the question.
