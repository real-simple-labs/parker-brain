# Process — Reaction Hooks

Open on a strong, genuine emotional reaction — tears, shock, anger, delight — held before any explanation, so the viewer has to keep watching to find out what caused it.

The format and its psychology live in `parker-system/creative-strategy-context/hooks.md`; that doc is the source of truth if the two ever drift. This is the execution playbook — how to write it well, not what it is.

## Psychological framework

- **Emotional contagion** — a strong reaction on screen triggers a reaction in the viewer.
- **Curiosity** — we have to know what caused the emotion.
- **Empathy activation** — we naturally care why someone is upset or thrilled.
- **Authenticity signal** — real emotion reads as real product impact.
- **Mirror neurons** — we subconsciously mirror the emotion we see.

## Required inputs

- A product moment that genuinely produces an emotional reaction — a first use, an unboxing, a result reveal. The reaction has to be earned by the product, not staged onto it.
- A creator who can react authentically rather than perform. Brief them to react, not to act.
- The emotion the moment produces and why it matches the product benefit.
- Brand voice and ICP.

## When to pick

- **Best for:** products with dramatic or impressive results. Emotional-connection categories — gifts, personal care, life improvement. Beauty and skincare especially. Gifting seasons. Partnership ads with authentic creators. Moments built around unboxing, first use, or a result reveal.
- **Worst for:** products with no moment that genuinely produces a reaction. Brands whose voice can't carry raw emotion. Anything where the only way to get the reaction is to fake it.

## Execution

1. **Find the real moment.** The reaction has to come from an actual product moment — first use, the result reveal, the unboxing. Brief the creator to react naturally to it, not to perform an emotion on cue.
2. **Open on the reaction, before the cause.** Lead with the emotion at full volume — tears welling, hand over mouth, the defensive-but-excited "people are mad at me." The cause is withheld; that gap is the hook.
3. **Hold the moment.** Stay on the reaction for a beat — two to three seconds — before cutting to explanation. Cutting away too fast kills the curiosity that the held emotion creates.
4. **Match the emotion to the benefit.** The feeling has to pay off into the product reason. Tears that resolve into a genuine result land; tears attached to nothing read as manipulation.
5. **Then explain and reveal.** Move from the held reaction into what caused it and the product, keeping the energy of the opening emotion intact.

## Output content

- The reaction being staged and the real moment that produces it.
- The opening framing if there is verbal or text — "why did I just cry trying this," "people are mad at me for sharing this."
- The held beat before the explanation.
- The transition from reaction into cause and product reveal.
- The ICP and the emotion being mirrored, and why it matches the benefit.
- The proven-pattern note tied to the reaction framework.

## What never to do

- Manufacture the emotion. A faked reaction is the fastest way to read as an ad and kill the format.
- Attach the reaction to a benefit it doesn't actually match.
- Cut away from the reaction before it has landed.
- Brief the creator to perform instead of react.
- Pull from the format's source examples in the library — those are for pattern recognition, not for output.
