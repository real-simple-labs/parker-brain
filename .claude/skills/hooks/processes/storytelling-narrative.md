# Process — Storytelling / Narrative Hooks

Open with the beginning of a story that creates intrigue and promises a journey with resolution. Uses narrative structure to hook attention and build emotional investment.

## Psychological framework

- **Dopamine release** — stories trigger anticipation; brain craves progression.
- **Narrative transportation** — we become absorbed in stories.
- **Emotional investment** — characters make us care.
- **Memory formation** — stories are more memorable than facts.
- **Completion desire** — must know how it ends.
- **Relatability** — we see ourselves in others' stories.

## Required inputs

- A real, specific moment that opens the story. "5 years ago my doctor told me..." or "In 2019 I lost..." Specific time markers and specific people.
- The arc of the story — where it goes from the opening moment.
- Brand voice — storytelling needs a register the brand can carry authentically.

## When to pick

- **Best for:** unaware audiences (do not yet know they have a problem). Building emotional connection. Complex customer journeys. Founder stories. Testimonials and case studies. Longer-form content. VSL openings.
- **Worst for:** quick-conversion product categories where the audience wants the offer fast. Short-form formats with limited time for setup.

## Execution

1. **Open in medias res.** Start in the middle of the story, not the beginning. "5 years ago my doctor told me I'd never run again" lands harder than "I've always been athletic."
2. **Promise a transformation arc.** The opening implies the journey to follow — from problem to discovery to resolution.
3. **Use perspective shifts.** Character learns something, discovers something, has an awakening. The shift is the dopamine.
4. **Build to product as resolution.** The product appears later in the story arc as the answer, not as the opening claim.
5. **Pacing.** 15-40 seconds of setup is appropriate for longer-form. Shorter formats need tighter pacing.

## Output content

- The exact opening line of the story.
- The arc the story will follow.
- Where the product enters in the arc.
- The visual progression — reflective opening, journey beats, resolution.
- The ICP and the emotional journey being targeted.
- The proven-pattern note.

## What never to do

- Generic story openings. Specific moments and specific stakes are the requirement.
- Make the product the immediate hero. Stories need an arc — the product as resolution lands harder than the product as the opening claim.
- Drag the setup past what the format can support.
- Use storytelling on impulse categories where the audience does not have time for a story.
