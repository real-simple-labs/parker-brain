# Process — Demographic / Qualification Hooks

Call out a specific demographic, age range, job title, lifestyle group, or identity in the opener. Immediately qualifies the audience and signals "this is for you."

The top-performing hook strategy for the Meta Andromeda era. Meta reads text overlays for targeting signals, so the identity callout doubles as a creative-metadata cue that improves delivery to the right audience.

## Psychological framework

- **Self-selection** — viewers immediately identify whether the ad is for them.
- **Targeting signal** — Meta uses creative metadata to deliver to the right audience.
- **Tribal identity** — creates belonging to a group.
- **Relevance filter** — qualifies ideal customers, filters out the rest.

## Required inputs

- The brand's primary persona — by identity, not just demographic age.
- Specific identity markers that the audience self-identifies with — job, lifestyle stage, body type, life situation.
- Brand voice — the callout language must match the brand's voice.

## When to pick

- **Best for:** broad targeting on Meta. Top-of-funnel content. Products with distinct customer personas. Andromeda-era creative where identity callouts replace narrow targeting. Near-universal application across categories.
- **Worst for:** brands where the persona is genuinely vague or multi-segment. Categories where calling out demographics would feel uncomfortable for the audience.

## Execution

1. **Lock the specific identity.** Not "women" — "working moms with kids under 5." Not "older men" — "men over 50 tired of looking older than they feel." Specificity is the strategy.
2. **Pick the callout phrasing.** "If you're between 25-35," "For working moms who," "Guys over 50" — direct identity language.
3. **Match the visual to the identity.** Creator who fits the demographic, setting that signals the lifestyle, b-roll that the demographic recognizes.
4. **Direct the text overlay.** Meta reads text overlays for targeting. Bold, prominent placement of the identity callout.

## Output content

- The exact identity callout — text overlay and voiceover.
- The specific persona this is built for, with the signals that define them.
- The visual cues that reinforce the identity match.
- The proven-pattern note tied to Andromeda-era targeting.

## What never to do

- Generic demographic callouts. "If you're a woman" is too broad.
- Identity callouts that the persona would not actually claim. People do not self-identify with marketer-imposed labels.
- Disrespectful or stereotyped callouts. The callout must feel respectful and authentic.
- Identity language the brand would not actually use.
