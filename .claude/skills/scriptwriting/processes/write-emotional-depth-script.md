# Process — Write Emotional-Depth Script

Script built to land at a specific level of emotional depth. The three levels are surface, behavioral, and identity. The deeper the level the script reaches, the more it converts — but the harder it is to write authentically.

## The three levels of emotional depth

### Level 1 — Surface
The obvious physical problem or sensation. Easy to describe. Most generic ad scripts stay here.
- Example phrasing: "I have dandruff flakes on my shoulders."

### Level 2 — Behavioral
What the audience cannot do because of the problem. The impact on daily life.
- Example phrasing: "I can't wear dark suits without constantly brushing my shoulders."

### Level 3 — Identity
How the problem makes the audience feel about themselves. Who they become because of it. What it means about them.
- Example phrasing: "I feel unprofessional. Like people think I don't take care of myself."

## When to pick

- **Best for:** problem/solution brands where the VoC reveals Level 2 and Level 3 depth. Solution-aware audiences. High-sophistication markets. Categories where the surface symptom is well-known but the identity dimension is under-leveraged.
- **Worst for:** lifestyle brands (use the storytelling or identity-signal approaches instead). Categories without genuine identity stakes.

## Execution

1. **Audit VoC for emotional depth.** What level do customers describe their experience at? Reviews and comments often go to Level 2 and 3 ("I avoided going out," "I felt embarrassed") — those phrases are the script's anchor language.
2. **Pick the target level.** Level 2 or Level 3 outperforms Level 1. Aim for Level 3 when the VoC supports it.
3. **Structure the script around the depth arc.** Open at Level 2 or Level 3 (immediate identity hit). Body deepens the identity stakes. Solution reveals the identity restoration.
4. **Anchor language in VoC.** Every line at Level 2 or Level 3 should pull from real customer phrases. Invented identity language reads as marketer-speak.
5. **Close with identity restoration.** The product is not just relief from the symptom — it is restoration of who the buyer wants to be.

## Output content

- The full script with timing.
- Visual cues per beat.
- The target emotional depth level with rationale.
- VoC sources for each Level 2 or Level 3 phrase.

## What never to do

- Invent identity-level language. The VoC has to support it.
- Stay at Level 1 when the VoC reveals deeper material.
- Identity-depth scripts on categories where the buyer is genuinely just buying a surface fix.
- Force Level 3 framing on brands without the customer language to back it up.
