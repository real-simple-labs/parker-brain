# Process — Script From Founder Story

The origin or why-narrative becomes the script — within what is publicly verified. When the founder story is documented in canon, it is the most trust-rich spine the brand owns. When the founder is absent from canon, the process says so and routes to intake.

A founder story converts because it is real. The discipline is never writing one the brand cannot verify.

## Required inputs

- The founder story as documented in the brand profile. Source: `sub-context-docs/operations-and-team.md` and the always-loaded narrative at `sub-context-docs/brand-profile-narrative.md` (brain root).
- Brand baseline — the brand's existing scripts.
- Brand profile, ICP, compliance constraints.

## Philosophy

A founder's origin story carries trust nothing else in the brand can match. The why behind the product — the problem the founder lived, the frustration that started it — is a narrative spine that earns belief because it is a real person's account. It works best for unaware and problem-aware audiences who buy into the brand before the product.

The hard constraint is verification. A founder story only works if it is true, and only the publicly documented version is usable. Inventing or embellishing a founder narrative is the fastest way to break trust and create a claim the brand cannot stand behind. If canon does not contain the founder story, this process does not guess — it names the gap and routes to expert-signal intake to source the real account.

## When to pick

- The founder story is documented in canon.
- Unaware or problem-aware audiences who buy the brand before the product.
- Longer-form or founder-led placements where a why-narrative has room.

## Execution

### Step 1 — Verify the founder story is in canon
Check the brand profile for the documented founder narrative. If it is absent or thin, stop — do not invent. Name the gap and route to expert-signal intake at `skills/expert-signal-intake/` to source the real story.

### Step 2 — Pull the verified narrative
From canon, pull the documented origin: the problem the founder lived, the frustration that started the brand, the why. Use only what is verified.

### Step 3 — Build the why-narrative
Hook opens on the founder's problem or moment. Body carries the origin. Close connects the why to the product and the audience.

### Step 4 — Source supporting language from VoC
The founder's voice anchors the narrative, but the audience's language describes the problem. Pull it from real reviews and comments.

### Step 5 — Brand baseline and compliance pass
Match pacing and CTA. Confirm every detail of the story is verified and every claim defensible.

## What good looks like

- The narrative uses only the publicly verified founder story.
- The why connects cleanly to the product and the audience's problem.
- When canon lacks the story, the process names the gap and routes to intake rather than inventing.
- Pacing and CTA match the brand baseline.

## Common failures

- Inventing or embellishing a founder story canon does not support.
- Writing the founder narrative when the founder is absent from canon, instead of routing to intake.
- Making the story about the brand instead of the audience's problem.
- An unverified detail or claim slipping in.
