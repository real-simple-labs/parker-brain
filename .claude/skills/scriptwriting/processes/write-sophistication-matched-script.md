# Process — Write Sophistication-Matched Script

Script matched to the customer's market sophistication level. High-sophistication and low-sophistication audiences need fundamentally different scripts even when targeting the same product.

## Required inputs

- The audience's market sophistication — has the audience tried many solutions or none? How crowded is the brand's competitive set in the audience's awareness?
- Brand profile, ICP, voice of customer.
- Format length.

## High sophistication

Audience has tried 3+ solutions. Skeptical, burned before. They have seen the standard pitches and stopped responding to them. The script must show that the brand understands their journey.

### Approach

- **Lead with acknowledgment.** "If you've tried X, Y, and Z and nothing's worked..." The script opens with a nod to their failed attempts.
- **Mechanism differentiation.** Why does this work when the others did not? Specificity is the lever. Generic "advanced formula" claims fail.
- **Lean into the specific failure modes the audience has experienced.** Pull from VoC the exact ways prior solutions disappointed.
- **Use myth-busting, contrarian claims, hidden-villain reveals.** The audience needs to understand why the standard approach is wrong.
- **Proof must be specific.** Clinical, expert, or detailed testimonials from people with similar journeys.

## Low sophistication

Audience has tried 0-2 things. Discovering the problem or just starting to look for solutions. The script must educate clearly.

### Approach

- **Lead with the problem named in simple language.** No jargon, no marketing-for-marketers copy.
- **Explain the mechanism in plain English.** Use metaphors that make abstract concepts concrete.
- **Use comparisons that simplify the choice.** "Old way vs new way."
- **Proof can be lighter.** Social proof and basic testimonials work — the audience does not yet have the failed-attempts skepticism of the high-sophistication group.
- **Pacing is forgiving.** The audience has not seen the standard pitches yet, so the standard pitches work.

## Execution

1. **Determine the sophistication level from brand context and VoC.** Look at how customers describe their journey before finding the brand. Many failed attempts → high sophistication. New to the problem → low sophistication.
2. **Pick the matched approach above.**
3. **Write the script in the customer's sophistication-appropriate language.** High-sophistication audiences want insider language. Low-sophistication audiences want plain explanation.
4. **Verify the proof depth matches.** High-sophistication needs specific clinical or expert proof. Low-sophistication is fine with social proof and basic testimonials.

## Output content

- The full script with timing.
- Visual cues per beat.
- The sophistication level targeted with rationale.
- The mechanism differentiation (for high-sophistication) or the educational layer (for low-sophistication).

## What never to do

- Write a high-sophistication script for a low-sophistication audience. They will not understand the references to past failures.
- Write a low-sophistication script for a high-sophistication audience. They will scroll past the basic education.
- Use generic "advanced formula" language with high-sophistication audiences. They have heard it from every competitor.
