# Process — Script From Customer Ritual

Film the ritual the community already performs. Customers invent their own demos and tests — the squat test, the drain check, the belt hack. The script choreographs the ritual the audience already does, honestly.

The ritual is real and self-invented, which is why it is credible. The script does not stage a demo; it documents one the community already trusts.

## Required inputs

- A documented customer-invented ritual or demo. Source: the brand's own review and comment reads — `source-pulls/customer-reviews.md` and `source-pulls/ad-comments.md` at the brain root, the newest `customer-review-audit` under `audits/`, or fresh pulls through the review/comment MCP surfaces — read through the method at `parker-system/creative-strategy-context/customer-review-mining-method.md`.
- Brand baseline — the brand's existing scripts.
- Brand profile, ICP, compliance constraints.

## Philosophy

The strongest demo is the one the customer invented. When a community independently develops a way to test or use a product — squatting in the jeans to check the stretch, running the drain test, rigging the belt a certain way — that ritual carries trust no brand-built demo can match, because the audience already knows it as theirs. Filming it is not a pitch; it is documentation of a behavior the community already performs.

The discipline is honesty. The script choreographs the real ritual exactly as customers do it, with the real outcome. Staging a ritual to produce a result it does not actually produce breaks the trust that made the ritual worth filming in the first place.

## When to pick

- A customer-invented ritual or demo is documented in reviews or comments.
- The ritual produces an honest, filmable outcome.
- Any awareness stage — the ritual is recognizable to the community.

## Execution

### Step 1 — Pull the documented ritual
From reviews and comments, pull the ritual customers actually perform and how they describe it. Confirm it is community-invented, not brand-suggested.

### Step 2 — Choreograph the real ritual
Map the ritual to beats exactly as customers do it. The hook is the setup, the body is the performance, the proof is the honest outcome.

### Step 3 — Keep the customer's framing
The ritual is named and described in the community's words, not rebranded into marketing language. If the community calls it the squat test, the script calls it the squat test.

### Step 4 — Honesty pass
Confirm the filmed outcome is the real outcome. Never choreograph the ritual to fake a result. If the honest result is modest, the script shows the modest result.

### Step 5 — Brand baseline and compliance pass
Match pacing and CTA. Confirm the ritual implies no claim the brand cannot defend.

## What good looks like

- The script documents a real community-invented ritual, in the community's words.
- The choreography matches how customers actually do it.
- The outcome shown is the honest outcome.
- Pacing and CTA match the brand baseline.

## Common failures

- Filming a brand-built demo and passing it off as a community ritual.
- Rebranding the ritual into marketing language and losing the community recognition.
- Staging the ritual to fake a result it does not produce.
- A claim the brand cannot defend riding on the ritual's outcome.
