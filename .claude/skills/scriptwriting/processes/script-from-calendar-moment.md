# Process — Script From Calendar Moment

A seasonal or campaign moment becomes the script's occasion. The marketing-calendar doc names the moment — the brand's own promo MO, a gifting occasion in the right voice, a launch beat. The script is built to land on that date in that register.

The moment supplies urgency and context the script does not have to manufacture. The calendar doc supplies the brand's own way of running it.

## Required inputs

- A named calendar moment with the brand's documented MO for it. Source: the marketing-calendar doc at `parker-system/prompts/brand-profile/marketing-calendar-and-campaigns.md`.
- Brand baseline — the brand's existing scripts.
- Brand profile, ICP, voice of customer, compliance constraints.

## Philosophy

A calendar moment is borrowed urgency. A seasonal occasion, a promo window, a launch — each carries built-in context and a reason to act now that the script does not have to build from scratch. The marketing-calendar doc records how the brand actually runs these moments: its promo MO, the days-out flash cadence, which occasions it plays and in what voice. The script inherits that MO rather than inventing one.

The discipline is voice fit per occasion. A gifting moment is not written to the gift; it is written in the voice of the person buying it — a Father's Day script in the daughter's voice, not the brand's. A promo flash matches the brand's own days-out cadence. The occasion sets the register, and the register is specific.

## When to pick

- A calendar moment is approaching and documented in the marketing-calendar doc.
- The brand has a known MO for the moment.
- The campaign is tied to a date — promo, gifting, launch, season.

## Execution

### Step 1 — Pull the moment and the brand's MO
From the marketing-calendar doc, pull the moment and how the brand runs it — promo cadence, gifting voice, launch beat. The MO is the brief.

### Step 2 — Set the occasion's register
A promo flash carries days-out urgency. A gifting moment is written in the buyer's voice about the recipient. A launch leads with the new thing's magic moment.

### Step 3 — Build the script on the occasion
Hook names or evokes the moment. Body holds the occasion's register. CTA carries the moment's urgency — the days-out flash, the gifting deadline, the launch window.

### Step 4 — Source language from VoC
Even on a calendar beat, the language is the customer's. The gift-buyer's voice and the promo-shopper's words come from real reviews and comments.

### Step 5 — Brand baseline and compliance pass
Match pacing and CTA to the baseline. Confirm any promo claim or deadline is accurate and defensible.

## What good looks like

- The script lands the named moment in the brand's documented MO.
- Gifting moments are in the buyer's voice, not the brand's.
- Promo urgency matches the brand's own days-out cadence.
- Pacing and CTA match the brand baseline.

## Common failures

- Inventing a promo cadence the brand does not run, instead of using its documented MO.
- Writing a gifting script in the brand voice instead of the buyer's voice.
- An inaccurate deadline or promo claim. Offer details must be defensible.
- Forcing a moment the brand has no MO for.
