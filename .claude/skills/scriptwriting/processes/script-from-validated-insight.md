# Process — Script From Validated Insight

The open-loops payoff. A validated hypothesis becomes the claim spine of the script. The script is the research finding dramatized — and every claim is already validated before a word is written.

The insight came out of the open-loops pipeline carrying its own evidence. The script does not have to argue; it has to dramatize.

## Required inputs

- A validated hypothesis from the open-loops pipeline. Source: `parker-system/prompts/open-loops/open-loops-roll-up.md` and the open-loops core block at `parker-system/prompts/_open-loops-core-block.md`.
- The evidence that validated it.
- Brand baseline — the brand's existing scripts.
- Brand profile, ICP, voice of customer, compliance constraints.

## Philosophy

Most scripts have to earn their central claim inside the ad. A validated-insight script does not — the claim arrived pre-proven from the research pipeline, with the evidence attached. That changes the writing job from persuasion to dramatization. The finding is the spine; the script is the dramatization of the finding for an audience that has never seen the research.

The discipline is the inverse of the usual claim-fidelity worry. Here the claim is already validated, so the risk is not fabrication — it is under-using the proof. The script should let the evidence carry weight, told as story rather than recited as a stat.

## When to pick

- A hypothesis has been validated through the open-loops pipeline with evidence.
- The validated finding is strong enough to be a script's central claim.
- Solution-aware or product-aware audiences who respond to a substantiated claim.

## Execution

### Step 1 — Pull the validated hypothesis and its evidence
From the open-loops roll-up, pull the finding and the evidence that validated it. Confirm it cleared validation — an untested hypothesis is not this process.

### Step 2 — Make the finding the claim spine
The validated finding is the script's central claim. The hook teases it, the body builds to it, the proof delivers it.

### Step 3 — Dramatize, do not recite
Tell the finding as story. The evidence is storytold — woven into a customer's experience or a demonstration — not read out as a bullet. A validated stat lands harder inside a scene than in a list.

### Step 4 — Source the language from VoC
The dramatization speaks in customer language even though the claim came from research. The finding is the brand's; the words are the customer's.

### Step 5 — Brand baseline and compliance pass
Match pacing and CTA. The claim is validated, but confirm the way it is stated stays inside the brand's compliance envelope.

## What good looks like

- The validated finding is the clear central claim.
- The evidence is dramatized into story, not recited.
- Every claim traces to the open-loops validation.
- Customer language throughout; pacing matches the brand baseline.

## Common failures

- Building on an unvalidated hypothesis. Validation is the gate.
- Reciting the finding as a stat instead of dramatizing it.
- Over-claiming past what the evidence actually validated.
- Stating a validated claim in language that breaks compliance phrasing.
