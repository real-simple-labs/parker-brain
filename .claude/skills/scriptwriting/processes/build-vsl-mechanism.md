# Process — Build VSL Mechanism

Sub-process used inside write-vsl. Builds the unique mechanism that differentiates the product. The mechanism is what justifies the price, the claim, and the reason this works when other solutions did not.

## Three components of a VSL mechanism

### Unique Mechanism Position (UMP)
The category-level claim. What space does this mechanism occupy in the market? Why does it deserve its own name? UMP positions the product as a new category, not just a better version of an existing one.

### Unique Mechanism Statement (UMS)
The specific claim about how the mechanism works. The scientific or process-based explanation. What does the product actually do that competitors do not?

### Delivery system
How the buyer experiences the mechanism. The form factor, the ritual, the daily use pattern. The delivery system makes the abstract mechanism concrete and ownable.

## Required inputs

- The product's actual mechanism — verified, not invented.
- Competitor positioning — what space they occupy.
- Compliance constraints on how the mechanism can be described.

## When to pick

- **Always run inside a VSL.** Mechanism is non-negotiable in VSLs. Without one, the script reads as another standard pitch.
- **Optionally run for non-VSL scripts** where mechanism differentiation is the key persuasive lever (high-sophistication markets, scientific products).

## Execution

1. **Identify the actual mechanism.** What does the product do at the chemical, biological, or process level? Pull from product team, ingredient docs, clinical research, or founder explanation.
2. **Position the mechanism in the market.** What category does it occupy? Is it a recognized space or a new one? If new, give it a proprietary name the brand can own.
3. **Write the UMP.** Category-level positioning statement. "The first X that does Y." "A new approach to Z."
4. **Write the UMS.** The specific how-it-works statement. Compliance-checked. Specific enough to differentiate from competitors but accurate to the science.
5. **Define the delivery system.** Form factor, ritual, daily use. How does the buyer experience the mechanism in their life?
6. **Test against competitor positioning.** If a major competitor could claim the same UMP or UMS, the mechanism is not differentiated enough.

## Output content

- The UMP statement.
- The UMS statement.
- The delivery system description.
- The compliance-checked language for the mechanism reveal section of the VSL.
- Why this mechanism positioning is defensible vs competitors.

## What never to do

- Invent a mechanism that does not exist. Compliance and credibility risk.
- Use vague mechanism claims ("advanced formula"). Specificity is the lever.
- Claim a UMP a competitor already owns or could credibly claim.
- Skip the delivery system. The abstract mechanism needs a concrete daily experience.
