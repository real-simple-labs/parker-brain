# Process — Write Natural CTA

Build the close of a script in conversational recommendation voice rather than generic ad voice. The CTA is the moment the script asks for the action — done badly, it sounds like an ad and breaks the trust the body built.

## Required inputs

- The full script context — what came before the CTA.
- The action being asked for — purchase, click, sign up, learn more.
- Brand voice.
- Any offer details — discount, guarantee, free shipping, bonus.

## When to pick

- **Always run when closing a script.** Every script ends in a CTA. This process can run as a sub-step at the end of any other scriptwriting process.

## The principle

People take recommendations from friends, not from ads. The CTA's job is to read as a recommendation. "If this sounds like you, I'd give it a shot" lands harder than "Click below to buy now."

## CTA structures

- **Conditional recommendation.** "If [audience attribute], try [product]."
- **Personal endorsement.** "I'd start with [product]. It's the one I'd recommend."
- **Low-friction nudge.** "Worth checking out at [domain]."
- **Risk reversal.** "There's free shipping and free returns. You've got nothing to lose."
- **Offer-led close.** "Use code X for $Y off — link in the description."

## Execution

1. **Match the CTA tone to the body's tone.** A casual conversational body needs a casual conversational CTA. A more authoritative body can carry a more direct CTA.
2. **Lean into recommendation voice.** "I'd give it a shot" and similar reads as personal advice.
3. **Add risk reversal if available.** Free shipping, free returns, money-back guarantee — these reduce friction and read as the brand standing behind the product.
4. **One CTA only.** Multiple competing CTAs split attention.
5. **Specific action with a clear next step.** "Link in the description," "tap below," "code at checkout."
6. **Match the offer detail to the script.** If the script anchored around urgency, the CTA echoes urgency. If it anchored around quality, the CTA echoes quality.

## Output content

- The exact CTA line.
- The offer details surfaced if applicable.
- The risk reversal if applicable.
- Why this CTA voice matches the script's body voice.

## What never to do

- Generic ad voice CTAs ("Click here now!").
- Multiple CTAs in a single script.
- CTAs that mismatch the body's tone.
- Skip the risk reversal when free shipping or returns are available — they meaningfully reduce friction.
- Promise an offer detail the brand cannot actually deliver.
