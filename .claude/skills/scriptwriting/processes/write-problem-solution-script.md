# Process — Write Problem-Solution Script

The classic direct-response framework. Name the problem, agitate the pain, present the solution, prove it, close. Most common pattern for problem/solution brands with problem-aware or solution-aware audiences.

## Required inputs

- Brand profile, ICP, voice of customer.
- The specific problem the script addresses.
- The product's mechanism — how it actually solves the problem.
- Proof points — testimonials, results, credentials, ingredients.
- Format length and placement.

## When to pick

- **Best for:** problem/solution brands. Problem-aware or solution-aware audiences. Categories where the pain is specific and the solution is direct.
- **Worst for:** unaware audiences who do not yet know the problem. Lifestyle brands where pain is not the lever. Premium brands where pain agitation feels off-brand.

## Structure

The script follows this beat structure. Adjust timing to the total runtime.

1. **Hook (0-3 seconds).** Demographic callout, problem statement, scam hook, or investment framing. Whatever stops the right scroller.
2. **Problem naming and agitation (3-10 seconds).** Name the problem in the customer's language. Go deep — Level 2 or 3 emotional depth. What the audience cannot do because of this, how it makes them feel about themselves.
3. **Mechanism reveal (10-20 seconds).** What the product actually does. The specific mechanism. Why it works when other things have not.
4. **Proof (20-35 seconds).** Testimonial, result, credential, demonstration. Visual proof if possible.
5. **CTA (35-end).** Specific, urgent, low-friction. One CTA.

## Execution

1. **Pull the problem language from VoC.** The audience must hear their own words in the script.
2. **Pick the agitation depth.** For problem-aware audiences, agitate moderately. For solution-aware audiences, lean into failed-attempt acknowledgment.
3. **Reveal the mechanism with specificity.** Vague mechanisms ("our advanced formula") fail. Specific mechanisms ("the prebiotic fiber that feeds the gut bacteria you've been missing") land.
4. **Pair proof with the agitated pain.** If the agitation was about embarrassment, the proof should be social-acceptance focused. If about discomfort, the proof should be relief-focused.
5. **Write the CTA in natural recommendation voice.** Not "click below now" — "if this sounds like you, I'd give it a shot."

## Output content

- The full script with timing.
- Visual cues per beat.
- The hook details (four hook elements).
- The CTA.
- VoC sources for the key language anchors.

## What never to do

- Agitate pain on brands without genuine emotional pain points. Problem agitation on a snack brand is nonsensical.
- Skip the mechanism. The audience needs to know WHY it works, not just THAT it works.
- Marketing voice. Customer language only.
- Multiple CTAs.
