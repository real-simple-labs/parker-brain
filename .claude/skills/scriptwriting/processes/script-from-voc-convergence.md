# Process — Script From VoC Convergence

A voice-of-customer convergence becomes the script's grammar. When dozens of customers independently land on the same image — the household meter, the squat test, the forever-bill — that convergence is the script's vocabulary, and the script speaks in nothing else.

The convergence is proof the language already lives in the customer's head. The script borrows it whole.

## Required inputs

- A documented VoC convergence with mention count. Source: the VoC assembly at `parker-system/prompts/voice-of-customer/voice-of-customer-assembly.md` and the metaphor and pain-phrase corpora.
- Brand baseline — the brand's existing scripts.
- Brand profile, ICP, compliance constraints.

## Philosophy

When a phrase or image converges — many customers reaching the same metaphor with no prompting — it has stopped being one person's words and become the category's shared language. That convergence is the strongest possible script grammar because the audience already thinks in it. A script built on a convergence does not have to teach the audience a new frame; it names the frame they already carry, and recognition does the persuading.

The discipline is purity. The script speaks ONLY in the convergence's documented vocabulary. No marketer synonyms, no adjacent phrasing the data does not support. If the customers say "squat test," the script says squat test, not "flexibility check."

## When to pick

- A VoC convergence is documented with a real mention count.
- The convergence carries an image or ritual the script can build around.
- Any awareness stage — the convergence meets the audience where their language already is.

## Execution

### Step 1 — Lock the convergence
Pull the converged phrase or image and its mention count from the VoC assembly. Confirm it is a genuine convergence — many independent customers, not one loud reviewer.

### Step 2 — Build the script's grammar from it
The convergence sets the script's entire vocabulary. The hook names it. The body lives inside it. The proof demonstrates it. Every beat uses the documented words.

### Step 3 — Stage the image or ritual
If the convergence is a ritual — the squat test, the drain check — the script choreographs it. If it is an image — the household meter, the forever-bill — the script visualizes it.

### Step 4 — Purity pass
Strip any word the convergence data does not support. Every phrase must trace to documented customer language. Marketer synonyms get cut.

### Step 5 — Brand baseline and compliance pass
Match the brand's pacing and CTA. Confirm the convergence implies no claim the brand cannot defend.

## What good looks like

- The script's whole vocabulary traces to the documented convergence.
- The converged image or ritual is staged, not just named.
- Zero marketer synonyms — every phrase is the customer's.
- Pacing and CTA match the brand baseline.

## Common failures

- Building on a single loud review and calling it a convergence. Mention count is the gate.
- Diluting the converged phrase with synonyms the data does not support.
- Naming the convergence but never staging its image or ritual.
- Implying a claim the convergence does not actually support.
