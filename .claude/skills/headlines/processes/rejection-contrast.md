# Process — Rejection / Contrast Headlines

Define the brand by what it is NOT as much as what it IS. Tribal filtering — makes the right people feel "this is for me" and the wrong people feel excluded.

## Structural patterns

The canonical structures and proven examples for this pattern live in `lifestyle-headline-generator.md` under "Rejection & Contrast Language" (it also appears in `problem-solution-headline-writer.md` for problem/solution brands). That doc is the source of truth; pull the structures and examples from it. The shapes below orient the execution steps that follow.

- "Not your [mainstream]" — Not your grocery store popcorn.
- "Forget [what everyone else does]" — Forget everything you know about popcorn.
- "F*ck [the old way]" — F*ck office dress codes.
- "Stop [inferior behavior]" — Stop wasting money on cheap readers.
- "Replace [bad] with [our]" — Replace boring popcorn with [brand].
- "Ditch [inferior option]" — Ditch your cheap readers for [brand].
- "This is X. Not Y." — This is a meal. Not a protein shake.

## When to pick

- **Best for:** lifestyle brands. Crowded categories where pushing against the mainstream defines the brand. Audiences who want to signal sophistication or rebellion. Identity-led purchases.
- **Worst for:** brands where the audience is happy with the mainstream. Premium brands where attacking competitors reads as petty.

## Execution

1. **Identify the adversary.** What is the brand pushing against? The mainstream version, the inferior alternative, the boring default, the outdated practice.
2. **Pick the rejection structure.** Different structures land differently. "F*ck X" is harder than "Forget X" which is harder than "Not your X."
3. **Match brand voice.** "F*ck" works for some brands and not for others. Calibrate.
4. **Keep it under 10 words.**

## Output content

- The exact headline.
- The adversary being rejected.
- The ICP and tribal identity the rejection appeals to.
- Why this structure fits the brand's positioning.

## What never to do

- Pick an adversary too beloved by the audience to attack.
- Use harder rejection structures on brands whose voice does not support them.
- Generic rejections. The adversary has to be specific.
