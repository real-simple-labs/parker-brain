# Special Launch Contexts

Some launches carry an additional context layer that changes the brief on top of the scenario classification. These handle **in addition to** the scenario playbook, never instead of it. A gifting launch is still a Scenario A, B, or C launch; the gifting layer sits on top.

## Gifting and occasion launches

Mother's Day, Father's Day, holidays, anniversaries, birthdays — any launch where **the buyer is not the recipient.**

The persona shifts to the **gifter**, and the gifter's relationship with the brand may be very different from the recipient's. A personalized family portrait targeted at dads for Mother's Day is the clean illustration: the product is for moms, but the entire ad is written for dads who are buying it.

This is a persona shift, not a format choice. Run it through the scenario classification the same as any other launch — the gifter is the audience being classified in Question 2, which frequently turns what looked like a Scenario A launch into a Scenario C one, because the brand may have no relationship with the gifter at all.

**Interim treatment — a dedicated Gifting Ads knowledge doc is in progress and will become the invoke target when it lands.** Until then, these defaults carry the layer:

- **Write to the gifter persona,** in their language and against their anxieties. The gifter's problem is usually not the product's problem — it is "will they like it," "am I late," "does this look like I tried."
- **Lead with the recipient's reaction** — the joy, the surprise, the moment of opening. The gifter is buying an outcome they will witness, and the reaction *is* the product benefit from where they stand.
- **Emphasize ease of purchase.** Gifters disproportionately want the path of least resistance. Shipping timelines, gift wrapping, size-free choices, and anything that removes a decision are conversion levers here in a way they are not elsewhere.
- **Use the occasion deadline as a built-in urgency mechanic.** It is real urgency rather than manufactured scarcity, which makes it one of the few honest deadline plays available.

Deliberately not prescribed here: the ad format. Gifting works across many formats, and choosing one before the scenario and persona are settled over-indexes on surface. Pick the format from the account's proven performers in the diagnostic, as with any other launch.

## Giveaway or free product as the launch mechanic

A free SKU to new subscribers, a giveaway tied to the launch moment, a free gift with purchase.

**The promotion mechanic is the hook.** Lead with the free product, not the product's features. The customer needs to understand the offer before they will care about the product itself, and an ad that leads with product benefit and buries the mechanic wastes its first three seconds.

Awareness splits sharply by phase here:

- **Warm audience** (the subscriber list) — most-aware framing. They know the brand; the offer is the entire message.
- **Cold audience** — problem-aware framing. Someone who does not know the brand needs to learn why this product solves their problem before "free" means anything. Free is only compelling once the thing being given away is understood to be worth having.

## Multi-video launches with a unifying theme

When the ask is for the "overarching theme" or "through-line" across multiple launch videos, **do not write individual scripts first.**

Produce the through-line concept and how each video plays a role inside it. **The through-line is the asset; the videos are instances.** Writing scripts first produces a set of ads that share a topic rather than a system, and stitching a through-line onto them afterward never fully takes.

Common through-line shapes:

- A question the campaign keeps answering
- A setting that recurs across every video
- A recurring character or voice
- A visual device that stitches the videos together
- A recurring on-screen text mechanic

Once the through-line is locked, each individual video script becomes a much easier deliverable — the hard decisions are already made, and the per-video brief is just its role inside the system.

## Layering

More than one of these can apply, and they compose with the scenario rather than overriding it. A holiday bundle launch to existing customers is Scenario B (bundle, new ritual), gifting layer (buyer is not recipient), possibly multi-video (a campaign, not one ad). Run the scenario playbook, then apply each layer's adjustments, then check that the result still reads as one coherent ad rather than three briefs stapled together.
