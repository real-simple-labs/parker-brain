# Launch Phase Sequencing

A new launch is rarely one ad. This process maps the deliverable to its phase and keeps two variables from collapsing into each other.

## Phase is not awareness stage

The most common confusion in launch planning. **Phase is about who is in the audience pool. Awareness stage is about what they already know.** They correlate — a warm pool skews most-aware — but they are different variables, and an ad pitched at the wrong depth fails even when the phase is right.

A warm-phase ad to a lapsed buyer who never bought the hero product is warm by pool and product-aware by knowledge. A cold-phase ad to someone who has been served the brand's content for months is cold by pool and further along by knowledge. Set both, independently.

## Warm-audience recapture

The low-lift first move, and the default opening play for Scenarios A and B.

Repeat buyers and engaged audiences are the lowest-lift, highest-likelihood first buyers of the new SKU, and the most likely source of the launch's early momentum. What this phase usually contains:

- **Email plus paid** to the existing customer list and engaged audience. The ad should feel continuous with the email — the customer should recognize one message, not two competing ones.
- **"Meet the new [SKU]" or "introducing [SKU]"** framing with a clear hero shot.
- **Founder or brand voice** explaining what is new and why existing customers in particular will care.
- **An introductory offer** tied to the launch moment, where the brand uses one.
- **Optionally, early or exclusive access** for hero-product owners.

**Two cautions.** Do not burn the warm list with an offer that is too aggressive or too frequent — a brand's best customers are also the most discount-sensitive, and the launch works better as a moment of access and recognition than as a markdown. And do not assume there is a discount at all; many launches have none, and the framing above works without one.

**Scenario C mostly skips this phase.** The brand's warm list is the *old* audience. If a meaningful slice of the new audience already sits in the list, that slice is worth a targeted warm ad; otherwise the launch starts cold.

## Cold-audience scaling

A distinct creative deliverable, not a re-cut of the warm ad. **The warm ad assumes the buyer knows the brand; the cold ad assumes they do not.** Plan for both and never let them blur — a cold ad carrying warm assumptions reads as an in-joke to an audience that was not there for the setup.

Cold scaling is where the scenario's angle set does most of its work, and where awareness stages below most-aware come into play.

## The arc

**Pre-launch / waitlist.** Tease, build anticipation, capture emails. Awareness skews problem-aware to product-aware. This is also the phase where beta-tester and early-user proof gets *created* — deciding to gather it here is what makes it available later, and discovering its absence during launch week is too late.

**Launch week.** Warm-audience recapture (intro offer, "meet the new") running alongside the first cold-audience scaling. Awareness skews most-aware for warm and solution-aware for cold.

**Post-launch evergreen.** Refine the winning angles, deepen scaling, retire the underperformers. Awareness stages span solution-aware through unaware as the audience widens.

## The failure mode

Most brands do one of two things: over-invest in launch week and have nothing for evergreen, or over-invest in evergreen and skip the launch moment entirely.

The right answer is almost always **distinct creative for each phase, mapped to the awareness stage that phase is actually reaching.** When the brief asks for "launch creative" without specifying, ask which phase — or produce for the phase the brief implies and say plainly which one it is, so the gap is visible rather than silent.

## Tagging

Every deliverable carries its phase in the Launch Context Summary. A script that does not say whether it is warm-phase or cold-phase leaves the strategist unable to tell whether it matches the brief, and leaves the media buyer unable to tell which audience to run it against.
